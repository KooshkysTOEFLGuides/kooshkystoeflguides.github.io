Vocabulary · Ranked study list

TOEFL Vocabulary Megalist

Thousands of words from well-known TOEFL and academic vocabulary resources, merged into one ranked Google Sheet.

What this is

TOEFL vocabulary resources overlap heavily, and it is not always obvious where to start. I collected the words from several major lists and books, cleaned them, merged duplicates, and ranked the result from higher priority to lower priority.

The current sheet contains roughly 3,000–4,000 words, including useful members of the same word families.

How words are ranked

  • Source credibility More established academic and TOEFL-focused sources receive more weight.
  • Agreement between sources A word appearing in several different lists receives a small boost.
  • Position inside tiered lists Earlier AWL sublists and easier or more essential tiers count more.
  • General English frequency More common words receive a frequency boost using a Zipf score.

The ranking is a study aid, not a claim that one exact score can measure the value of a word perfectly. Use it to decide what to study first.

How to read the columns

Score
The main priority score. Higher generally means study earlier.
Zipf
A logarithmic estimate of how common the word is in English.
Source columns
0 means absent. Tier numbers show where the word appeared inside that source.
AWL 1–10
Sublist 1 contains more common academic words; Sublist 10 is less common.
Family rows
Related derivational forms are placed after their main word where useful.
