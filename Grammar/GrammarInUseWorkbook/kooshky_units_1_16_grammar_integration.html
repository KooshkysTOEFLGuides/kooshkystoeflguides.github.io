    Overview

    What this pack trains

    This is not a tense worksheet. It is a time-control worksheet. The student should learn to move clearly between habits, current changes, finished events, ongoing experience, earlier past causes, and present results.

    present simple present continuous past simple past continuous present perfect present perfect continuous past perfect past perfect continuous
    Teacher principle: do not reward students for using every tense artificially. Reward them for choosing a tense because it clarifies time meaning.

    Exercise 1

    Time Meaning Warm-up

    Answer each question in 2–3 complete sentences. This is a soft diagnostic, so the goal is natural production first, then correction.

    1. What is something you usually do every day, but are not doing much these days?
    2. What is something you are currently trying to improve?
    3. What is something you did last weekend?
    4. What were you doing at 9 p.m. last night?
    5. What is something you have done many times in your life?
    6. What is something you have been doing recently that takes a lot of effort?
    7. How long have you been studying English seriously?
    8. What is something you had already learned before you started preparing for TOEFL?
    9. What is something you had been doing for a long time before you stopped?
    10. Tell me about a time when something interrupted what you were doing.

    Exercise 7

    Grammar Trap Choices

    Choose the best correction. Sometimes more than one answer is grammatically possible, but the meaning changes. Explain the difference.

    1. I have lived in Berlin in 2021.

    1. I lived in Berlin in 2021.
    2. I have lived in Berlin since 2021.
    3. I was living in Berlin since 2021.

    2. I was studying English for two years.

    1. I studied English for two years.
    2. I have studied English for two years.
    3. I have been studying English for two years.

    3. When I got home, my brother cooked dinner.

    1. When I got home, my brother was cooking dinner.
    2. When I got home, my brother had cooked dinner.
    3. When I got home, my brother has cooked dinner.

    4. I have been to the gym yesterday.

    1. I went to the gym yesterday.
    2. I have gone to the gym yesterday.
    3. I had gone to the gym yesterday.

    5. I had been nervous because I didn’t speak in public before.

    1. I was nervous because I hadn’t spoken in public before.
    2. I was nervous because I haven’t spoken in public before.
    3. I had been nervous because I wasn’t speaking in public before.

    6. She has worked here since three years.

    1. She has worked here for three years.
    2. She worked here for three years.
    3. She has worked here since 2023.

    7. I was knowing the answer.

    1. I knew the answer.
    2. I was learning the answer.
    3. I had known the answer.

    8. By the time I arrived, the movie started.

    1. By the time I arrived, the movie had started.
    2. By the time I arrived, the movie has started.
    3. By the time I arrived, the movie was starting.

    9. Recently, I improved my speaking a lot.

    1. Recently, I have improved my speaking a lot.
    2. Recently, I was improving my speaking a lot.
    3. Recently, I had improved my speaking a lot.

    10. I have been finishing my homework.

    1. I have finished my homework.
    2. I have been doing my homework.
    3. I finished my homework.
    Answer key
    1. A or B. A = only during 2021. B = from 2021 until now. C is wrong with since 2021.
    2. A, B, or C depending on meaning. A = finished two-year period. B = from past until now, neutral. C = from past until now, emphasizing activity/process.
    3. A or B depending on meaning. A = he was in the middle of cooking. B = dinner was already cooked.
    4. A. Use past simple with a finished time expression like yesterday.
    5. A. The lack of experience happened before the nervousness.
    6. A or C. A uses duration; C uses starting point. B means the period is finished.
    7. A. Know is normally a state verb.
    8. A. The movie started before I arrived.
    9. A. Recently often connects past change to the present.
    10. A or B. A = complete. B = activity continued for some time; completion is not emphasized.
