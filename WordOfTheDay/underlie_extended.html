Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 transitive verb /ˌʌn.dɚˈlaɪ/

To be the hidden cause, basis, or explanation for something.

Natural examples

  1. Several basic assumptions underlie the professor’s theory.
  2. Economic inequality may underlie many social problems.
  3. A strong desire for security underlies many political decisions.
  4. Biological processes underlie many forms of animal behavior.
  5. Cultural values often underlie the way people communicate.
  6. The researchers wanted to identify the factors that underlie student motivation.

Nuance & implication

This is the most TOEFL-relevant meaning. Underlie suggests that something is deeper, less visible, or more fundamental than the thing we can easily observe. It is often used in academic explanations: one thing underlies a trend, behavior, theory, disease, argument, or problem.

Grammar notes

Underlie is transitive, so it takes a direct object: X underlies Y. Do not use underlie to or underlie behind. The past tense is underlay, and the past participle is underlain. The adjective underlying is extremely common: the underlying cause, an underlying assumption.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The reason underlies behind the problem. → ✅ The reason underlies the problem.
  • ❌ Several assumptions underlie to this theory. → ✅ Several assumptions underlie this theory.
  • ❌ The theory is underlied by two ideas. → ✅ The theory is underlain by two ideas.

TOEFL tip

In Reading and Listening, underlie often signals a cause-and-effect relationship. If a passage says X underlies Y, X is usually the deeper reason or basis, and Y is the visible result, behavior, pattern, or argument.

Meaning 2 transitive verb /ˌʌn.dɚˈlaɪ/

To lie or be located under another thing, surface, or layer.

Natural examples

  1. A layer of clay underlies much of the farmland.
  2. Granite underlies large parts of the mountain range.
  3. The ancient settlement is underlain by older layers of human occupation.
  4. Thin layers of sand underlie the dark topsoil.
  5. In this region, limestone underlies the surface rock.
  6. The valley is underlain by volcanic material from an earlier eruption.

Nuance & implication

This literal meaning is common in academic topics such as geology, geography, archaeology, and environmental science. It means that one physical layer is below another layer. It is more formal and academic than simply saying is under.

Grammar notes

This meaning is also transitive: rock underlies the soil. In scientific writing, the passive form is common: the area is underlain by limestone. Remember the irregular forms: underlie → underlay → underlain.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The clay underlies under the soil. → ✅ The clay underlies the soil.
  • ❌ The region is underlied by limestone. → ✅ The region is underlain by limestone.
  • ❌ The rock is underlying by sand. → ✅ The rock is overlain by sand. / The sand overlies the rock.

TOEFL tip

In science passages, underlie may be literal rather than abstract. Look for nearby words such as rock, soil, sediment, layer, surface, valley, or region. These usually signal the physical meaning.

Word Family

underlying

Part of speech: adjective

Definition: deeper, more basic, or not immediately visible, but important as a cause or explanation.

  1. The underlying cause of the disease was difficult to identify.
  2. The study examined the underlying assumptions of the economic model.

underlay

Part of speech: past tense verb

Definition: the past tense form of underlie; it means was the basis of something or lay under something.

  1. A lack of trust underlay the conflict between the two groups.
  2. A thick layer of clay underlay the fertile soil.

underlain

Part of speech: past participle verb

Definition: the past participle form of underlie, often used in passive structures.

  1. The argument is underlain by several questionable assumptions.
  2. The region is underlain by limestone and shale.

Common Collocations

underlie a problem

Meaning: to be the deeper cause of a problem.

Example: Poor communication often underlies problems in large organizations.

underlie a theory

Meaning: to be one of the basic ideas that a theory is built on.

Example: Several assumptions about human behavior underlie this theory.

underlie an argument

Meaning: to form the basic reasoning behind an argument.

Example: The argument is underlain by the idea that education should be widely accessible.

underlie a trend

Meaning: to explain why a pattern is happening.

Example: Demographic change may underlie the trend toward smaller households.

underlie behavior

Meaning: to be the deeper reason for how people or animals act.

Example: Scientists studied the brain processes that underlie social behavior.

underlie a decision

Meaning: to be the hidden or basic reason for a choice.

Example: Economic pressure underlay the government’s decision to change the policy.

underlying cause

Meaning: the deeper cause that explains a problem or situation.

Example: Doctors must treat the underlying cause, not only the symptoms.

underlying factor

Meaning: an important factor that is not always obvious.

Example: The researchers identified stress as an underlying factor in the decline.

underlying assumption

Meaning: a basic belief or idea that supports an argument or plan.

Example: The underlying assumption is that people respond strongly to financial incentives.

underlying mechanism

Meaning: the process that explains how something works.

Example: The experiment helped reveal the underlying mechanism of memory formation.

be underlain by

Meaning: to have something as a deeper basis or as a physical layer beneath.

Example: The valley is underlain by volcanic rock.

principles underlying something

Meaning: basic ideas that support or explain something.

Example: Students should understand the principles underlying the scientific method.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

be the basis of form the basis of explain account for be beneath

Be the basis of and form the basis of are closest for the abstract meaning. Explain and account for focus more directly on cause. Be beneath is closest for the physical meaning, but it is less formal.

Direct antonyms

result from follow from be caused by overlie

For the abstract meaning, there is no perfect single-word antonym. If X underlies Y, then Y results from X. For the physical meaning, overlie is the most direct opposite: if sand overlies rock, the sand is above the rock.

Etymology

Underlie comes from the simple idea of under + lie: something “lies under” something else. This helps explain both modern meanings. A rock layer can literally lie under soil, and a cause or assumption can figuratively lie under a problem, theory, or argument.

Answer Key
  1. underlying — The adjective fits before cause.
  2. underlay — This is the past tense of underlie.
  3. underlain — The passive form is be underlain by.
  4. Correct — Underlie is transitive and takes a direct object.
  5. Incorrect — Say: The reason underlies the problem.
  6. Incorrect — Say: The region is underlain by volcanic rock.
  7. underlie — The factors explain or form the basis of motivation.
  8. underlying — The common collocation is underlying cause.
  9. underlying — The common collocation is underlying assumptions.
