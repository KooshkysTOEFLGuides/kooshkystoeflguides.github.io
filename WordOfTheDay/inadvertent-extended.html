Meanings and usage

The core definition and examples are always included. Additional notes appear only when they are accurate and useful.

Meaning 1

Unintended because of inattention

adjective /ˌɪnədˈvɝːtənt/ Common in formal, academic, legal, and professional English. It is less common in casual conversation than accidental or unintentional.

not intended or planned, usually because someone was not careful or did not notice what they were doing

In simpler words: unintended or accidental

Typical pattern: inadvertent + noun

Natural examples

  1. An inadvertent error in the report caused confusion among the researchers.
  2. The policy had the inadvertent effect of increasing housing costs.
  3. She made an inadvertent reference to information that was supposed to remain private.
  4. The company apologized for the inadvertent release of customers' personal data.
  5. His question caused inadvertent offense, although he had meant no harm.
  6. The experiment was affected by the inadvertent contamination of several samples.
  7. An inadvertent click deleted part of the document.
  8. The new rule may create inadvertent barriers for low-income applicants.

Nuance and implication

Central nuance: The word emphasizes that something happened without intention, often because a person failed to notice or think about a detail.

Usual implication: It often describes mistakes, harmful consequences, disclosures, omissions, or other unwanted results.

Compared with “accidental”: Accidental is broader and can describe any unplanned event. Inadvertent often suggests that the event resulted from inattention, lack of awareness, or failure to notice something.

Grammar notes

How to use it
Use it before a noun, as in an inadvertent mistake or an inadvertent disclosure.
What can follow it
It commonly comes before nouns such as error, mistake, omission, effect, consequence, disclosure, release, and violation.
Position in a sentence
It is usually used before a noun. Using it after be is possible, but less common. For example: The disclosure was inadvertent.

Useful patterns

an inadvertent + singular noun

Use this pattern for one unintended action, mistake, or result.

Example: An inadvertent omission changed the meaning of the sentence.

inadvertent + plural noun

Use this pattern when referring to several unintended mistakes or effects.

Example: The system was designed to prevent inadvertent errors.

the inadvertent effect of + noun or -ing form

Use this pattern to describe an unintended result.

Example: The change had the inadvertent effect of discouraging students from applying.

Common wrong usage

Wrong: He inadvertently mistake revealed the answer.

Correct: He inadvertently revealed the answer.

Use inadvertently as an adverb to describe how the action happened.

Wrong: The error was happened inadvertently.

Correct: The error happened inadvertently.

Do not use was before happened in this sentence.

Wrong: She made an inadvertent on the application form.

Correct: She made an inadvertent error on the application form.

Inadvertent is an adjective, so it normally needs a noun after it.

Wrong: The company deliberately made an inadvertent disclosure.

Correct: The company made an inadvertent disclosure.

Deliberately and inadvertent express opposite ideas, so they normally should not describe the same action.

adverb

inadvertently

/ˌɪnədˈvɝːtəntli/

without intending to do something, usually because of inattention or lack of awareness

Relationship: Inadvertently is the adverb form of inadvertent.

Pattern: inadvertently + verb; verb + inadvertently

Usage note: Use it to describe how an unintended action happened.

  1. The researcher inadvertently entered the same result twice.
  2. The email was inadvertently sent to the entire department.
  3. The policy may inadvertently disadvantage smaller businesses.

uncountable noun

inadvertence

/ˌɪnədˈvɝːtəns/

lack of attention that causes an unintended action or mistake

Relationship: Inadvertence is the noun form related to inadvertent.

Pattern: through inadvertence; result from inadvertence

Usage note: This noun is formal and much less common than inadvertent and inadvertently.

  1. The information was omitted through inadvertence rather than dishonesty.
  2. The error appears to have resulted from simple inadvertence.

Synonyms and antonyms

Close synonyms

unintentional

adjective · Unintended because of inattention

Shared meaning: Both words describe something that was not done deliberately.

Difference: Unintentional simply means not intended. Inadvertent often adds the idea that the action happened because someone did not notice something or was not sufficiently careful.

Example: The damage was unintentional, but the company still accepted responsibility.

accidental

adjective · Unintended because of inattention

Shared meaning: Both words can describe an unplanned event.

Difference: Accidental is more common and broader. It can describe events caused by chance, while inadvertent often focuses on human inattention or lack of awareness.

Example: The fire was caused by the accidental ignition of nearby materials.

unintended

adjective · Unintended because of inattention

Shared meaning: Both words describe results or actions that were not planned.

Difference: Unintended focuses on the absence of intention and is especially common for effects and consequences. Inadvertent more strongly suggests that someone overlooked or failed to notice something.

Example: The reform produced several unintended consequences.

Direct or useful antonyms

deliberate

adjective · Unintended because of inattention

Opposition: Deliberate describes an action that is done intentionally and after conscious thought.

Usage note: Deliberate is a direct opposite when discussing whether an action was intended.

Example: The removal of the paragraph was a deliberate editorial decision.

intentional

adjective · Unintended because of inattention

Opposition: Intentional means planned or done on purpose.

Usage note: Intentional is a neutral and direct opposite of inadvertent.

Example: Investigators concluded that the damage was intentional.
