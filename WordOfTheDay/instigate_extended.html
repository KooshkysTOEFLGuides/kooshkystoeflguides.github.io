Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 transitive verb /ˈɪn.stə.ɡeɪt/

To make an event, process, action, or formal procedure start.

Natural examples

  1. The university instigated a review of its online learning policies.
  2. The mayor instigated a new program to reduce traffic in the city center.
  3. The accident instigated a broader investigation into workplace safety.
  4. New scientific evidence instigated changes in the treatment guidelines.
  5. The committee instigated legal proceedings after discovering financial irregularities.

Nuance & implication

This meaning is formal and often appears with official actions such as reviews, investigations, reforms, programs, or legal proceedings. It suggests that someone or something causes a process to begin, not simply that the process happens by itself.

Grammar notes

Instigate is normally transitive: you instigate something. Common objects include a review, an investigation, reform, change, legal proceedings, and new measures.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The school instigated about the problem. → ✅ The school instigated an investigation into the problem.
  • ❌ The new rule was instigated to students. → ✅ The new rule was introduced to students.

TOEFL tip

In TOEFL Reading, instigate may be close to initiate, start, or bring about. Check whether the object is a formal action or process.

Meaning 2 transitive verb /ˈɪn.stə.ɡeɪt/

To deliberately encourage, provoke, or cause a negative action, conflict, or trouble to begin.

Natural examples

  1. The leader was accused of instigating violence during the protest.
  2. Several online posts instigated a heated argument among community members.
  3. The rumor instigated panic in the neighborhood.
  4. Officials claimed that outside groups had instigated the unrest.
  5. Instead of calming the situation, his speech instigated further conflict.

Nuance & implication

This meaning usually has a negative tone. It often implies responsibility: the person who instigates violence, unrest, or an argument is not just present; they help cause it or encourage it.

Grammar notes

The most common pattern is instigate + negative event: instigate violence, instigate unrest, instigate a riot, instigate a conflict. A less common but possible pattern is instigate someone to do something, especially in formal or legal contexts.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The teacher instigated students to study harder. → ✅ The teacher encouraged students to study harder.
  • ❌ The argument instigated because of a small mistake. → ✅ The argument started because of a small mistake.

TOEFL tip

When instigate appears with words like violence, unrest, panic, rebellion, or conflict, the sentence usually has a negative implication.

Word Family

instigation

Part of speech: noun

Definition: the act of causing, encouraging, or starting something, especially something negative or formal

  1. The policy review began at the instigation of several faculty members.
  2. The police investigated his possible role in the instigation of the violence.

instigator

Part of speech: countable noun

Definition: a person who starts or encourages something, especially trouble, conflict, or a plan

  1. Officials tried to identify the main instigator of the unrest.
  2. She was not the instigator of the disagreement, but she made it worse.

Common Collocations

instigate an investigation

Meaning: to officially start an investigation

Example: The accident instigated an investigation into safety procedures.

instigate a review

Meaning: to start a formal process of examining something

Example: Student complaints instigated a review of the grading system.

instigate reforms

Meaning: to begin or cause changes that improve a system

Example: The economic crisis instigated major reforms in the banking sector.

instigate change

Meaning: to cause change to begin

Example: Public pressure can instigate meaningful change in environmental policy.

instigate legal proceedings

Meaning: to begin a legal process or court action

Example: The company instigated legal proceedings against the former employee.

instigate violence

Meaning: to encourage or cause violent behavior

Example: The speech was criticized for instigating violence.

instigate unrest

Meaning: to cause public disorder, protest, or conflict

Example: False information can instigate unrest during a crisis.

be accused of instigating

Meaning: to be blamed for starting or encouraging something bad

Example: Several activists were accused of instigating the protests.

at the instigation of someone

Meaning: because someone suggested, encouraged, or caused it

Example: The meeting was held at the instigation of the department chair.

the main instigator

Meaning: the person most responsible for starting something

Example: Investigators believed he was the main instigator of the attack.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

initiate provoke incite trigger set off

Initiate is more neutral and often positive or official. Provoke means to cause a reaction. Incite is very close to instigate when the action is negative, especially violence or unrest.

Direct antonyms

prevent stop discourage suppress calm

These words are used when someone blocks an event from starting, reduces conflict, or prevents people from taking action.

Etymology

Instigate comes from Latin instigare, meaning “to urge on” or “to provoke.” This history helps explain the modern meaning: when you instigate something, you push it forward and help make it begin.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. The police tried to find the main __________ of the violence.
  2. The review began at the __________ of several senior professors.
  3. The report may __________ major reforms in the education system.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The government instigated a review of its immigration policy.
  2. The teacher instigated the students to feel more confident.
  3. Several messages online were accused of instigating panic.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. The safety failure instigated __________ into the factory’s working conditions.
  2. The organization was accused of instigating __________ during the demonstration.
  3. Public pressure helped instigate __________ in the healthcare system.
Answer Key
  1. instigator — A person who starts or encourages something, especially trouble.
  2. instigation — The phrase is at the instigation of someone.
  3. instigate — The verb is needed before the object major reforms.
  4. Correct. Instigate a review is a natural formal collocation.
  5. Incorrect. Better: The teacher encouraged the students to feel more confident.
  6. Awkward/partly incorrect. Better: Several online messages were accused of instigating panic.
  7. an investigation — Instigate an investigation means to start an official inquiry.
  8. violence or unrest — Both are natural negative objects after instigate.
  9. reforms or change — Both fit the idea of starting improvements in a system.
