Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 noun · countable /ˈhaɪəˌrɑːrki/

A system in which people or groups are arranged in levels of power, status, or importance.

Natural examples

  1. The company has a strict hierarchy, so most decisions must be approved by senior managers.
  2. In many universities, professors, department chairs, and deans belong to different levels of the academic hierarchy.
  3. The new policy tried to reduce the hierarchy between managers and ordinary employees.
  4. Social hierarchies can affect who has access to education, wealth, and political influence.
  5. Biologists observed a dominance hierarchy among the animals in the group.

Nuance & implication

This meaning often suggests authority, rank, and unequal levels of power. A hierarchy can be useful because it makes responsibility clear, but it can also sound negative if it prevents communication or creates unfairness.

Grammar notes

Hierarchy is usually countable: a hierarchy, the hierarchy, several hierarchies. Common patterns include a hierarchy of managers, within the hierarchy, high in the hierarchy, and at the top/bottom of the hierarchy. It is a noun, not a normal verb, so use verbs like create, establish, maintain, reduce, or challenge.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The manager hierarchied the workers. → ✅ The manager created a hierarchy among the workers.
  • ❌ She is in a high hierarchy. → ✅ She is high in the hierarchy.

TOEFL tip

In reading passages, hierarchy often appears in discussions of society, institutions, workplaces, education, government, or animal behavior. Ask yourself: Who has more power? Who has less?

Meaning 2 noun · countable /ˈhaɪəˌrɑːrki/

An arrangement of things, ideas, categories, or needs from higher, more important, or more general levels to lower or more specific levels.

Natural examples

  1. The biology lecture explained the hierarchy from cells to tissues to organs and organ systems.
  2. Maslow's hierarchy of needs is often discussed in psychology courses.
  3. A strong academic essay needs a clear hierarchy of ideas, with main points supported by details.
  4. The website uses color, size, and spacing to create a clear visual hierarchy.
  5. Scientists organize living things in a hierarchy of categories, from broad groups to specific species.

Nuance & implication

This meaning is about structure and order, not necessarily power over people. It is common in academic contexts when information is classified, organized, or presented from general to specific or from most important to least important.

Grammar notes

Use a hierarchy of + plural noun: a hierarchy of ideas, a hierarchy of needs, a hierarchy of categories. You can say something is arranged in a hierarchy or organized into a hierarchy. The adjective is hierarchical, as in a hierarchical system. The adverb is hierarchically, as in The data is organized hierarchically.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The ideas are in hierarchy. → ✅ The ideas are arranged in a hierarchy.
  • ❌ The essay has a hierarchy very logic. → ✅ The essay has a very logical hierarchy of ideas.

TOEFL tip

When hierarchy appears in science or academic writing, look for levels: general to specific, basic to advanced, large to small, or most important to least important.

Word Family

hierarchy

Part of speech: noun

Definition: a system or structure with higher and lower levels of rank, power, importance, or generality

  1. The organization has a clear hierarchy of roles.
  2. The chart shows a hierarchy of categories.

hierarchical

Part of speech: adjective

Definition: arranged in levels, with higher and lower positions

  1. The company uses a hierarchical management structure.
  2. The researcher presented the data in a hierarchical model.

hierarchically

Part of speech: adverb

Definition: in a way that is arranged by levels or rank

  1. The files are organized hierarchically, from general folders to specific documents.
  2. The textbook presents the concepts hierarchically, beginning with the broadest categories.

Common Collocations

social hierarchy

Meaning: levels of status or power in a society

Example: The novel shows how the social hierarchy limited the choices of poor families.

organizational hierarchy

Meaning: the ranking of roles inside an organization

Example: The organizational hierarchy became clearer after the company published a new chart.

corporate hierarchy

Meaning: levels of authority in a company

Example: Employees lower in the corporate hierarchy often have less influence over major decisions.

strict hierarchy

Meaning: a system with very clear and strong levels of authority

Example: The military is known for its strict hierarchy and formal rules.

rigid hierarchy

Meaning: a hierarchy that is difficult to change

Example: A rigid hierarchy can stop new ideas from reaching decision-makers.

clear hierarchy

Meaning: a system whose levels are easy to understand

Example: The project needed a clear hierarchy of responsibilities.

hierarchy of needs

Meaning: an ordered list of human needs, from basic needs to higher goals

Example: Maslow's hierarchy of needs begins with basic physical needs like food and sleep.

hierarchy of ideas

Meaning: main ideas and supporting ideas arranged by importance

Example: Before writing the essay, she created a hierarchy of ideas.

visual hierarchy

Meaning: the way design guides attention from more important to less important information

Example: Large headings create a strong visual hierarchy on the page.

dominance hierarchy

Meaning: a ranking of animals in a group based on power or control

Example: The researchers studied the dominance hierarchy among the wolves.

at the top of the hierarchy

Meaning: in the highest position

Example: Senior administrators are at the top of the university hierarchy.

move up the hierarchy

Meaning: gain a higher position in a ranked system

Example: After several years, she moved up the hierarchy and became department director.

challenge the hierarchy

Meaning: question or oppose the existing order of power or status

Example: The reform movement challenged the hierarchy that had controlled the institution for decades.

establish a hierarchy

Meaning: create a system of levels or ranks

Example: The researchers established a hierarchy of categories to classify the data.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

ranking system chain of command power structure ordered structure classification system

Ranking system is the closest general synonym. Chain of command is used for authority in organizations or the military. Power structure focuses on who controls decisions. Ordered structure and classification system are closer to the second meaning, where ideas or categories are organized into levels.

Direct antonyms

equality flat structure egalitarian system random order disorder

Equality, flat structure, and egalitarian system contrast with hierarchy as a system of power or status. Random order and disorder contrast with hierarchy as an organized structure of ideas or categories.

Etymology

Hierarchy comes from Greek roots connected to sacred rule or priestly rank. It entered English through Latin and French. Over time, the meaning became broader: not only religious ranks, but any system arranged in higher and lower levels. This history can help you remember the modern meaning: a hierarchy is a structured order of levels.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. The company has a complicated __________, so simple decisions often require several approvals.
  2. The information is arranged __________, from broad categories to specific details.
  3. A __________ organization usually has clear levels of authority.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The professor asked us to create a hierarchy of ideas before writing the essay.
  2. The manager hierarchied the workers into three groups.
  3. Senior officials are at the top of the hierarchy.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. The designer made the title larger to improve the page's __________.
  2. The movement tried to challenge the old __________ and give ordinary citizens more power.
  3. The researchers studied the __________ among the animals in the group.
Answer Key
  1. hierarchy — A noun is needed after a complicated.
  2. hierarchically — An adverb is needed to describe how the information is arranged.
  3. hierarchical — An adjective is needed before organization.
  4. Correct. A hierarchy of ideas is a natural academic phrase.
  5. Incorrect. Better: The manager divided the workers into three levels in the hierarchy. Hierarchy is not normally used as a verb.
  6. Correct. At the top of the hierarchy is the natural phrase.
  7. visual hierarchy — This refers to how design guides attention.
  8. social hierarchy — This refers to levels of power or status in society.
  9. dominance hierarchy — This is common in animal behavior studies.
