Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 adjective /kəˈlektɪv/

Shared by, done by, or involving all the members of a group, not just one person.

Natural examples

  1. Solving environmental problems often requires collective action, not only individual choices.
  2. The team made a collective decision to repeat the experiment with better controls.
  3. The students felt a collective sense of relief after the final exam ended.
  4. The report argues that public health depends on collective responsibility.
  5. The workers' collective effort helped the organization finish the project ahead of schedule.

Nuance & implication

This meaning emphasizes the group as a whole. It often appears in academic writing about society, politics, history, psychology, education, teamwork, and environmental issues. It can suggest cooperation, shared responsibility, or group-level effects.

Grammar notes

Collective is usually an adjective before a noun: collective action, collective memory, collective responsibility, collective decision-making. It does not take a direct object because it is not a verb. Common patterns include collective responsibility for something, a collective effort to do something, and collective action against a problem.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The team collective solved the problem. → ✅ The team solved the problem collectively.
  • ❌ We need a collective to reduce pollution. → ✅ We need collective action to reduce pollution.

TOEFL tip

In TOEFL reading, collective often signals that the writer is discussing a group-level process. Ask: Is the text about one person, or about people acting together?

Meaning 2 adjective /kəˈlektɪv/

Belonging to or describing a group as a whole, especially when the group has a shared identity, memory, behavior, or interest.

Natural examples

  1. National holidays can strengthen a society's collective identity.
  2. The museum preserves the collective memory of the community.
  3. Researchers studied collective behavior among birds during migration.
  4. A crisis can change the collective mood of a city or country.
  5. The article discusses how stories shape a group's collective understanding of the past.

Nuance & implication

This meaning is close to Meaning 1, but it focuses less on action and more on shared qualities, experiences, or patterns. It is common in academic phrases such as collective memory, collective identity, collective behavior, and collective interest.

Grammar notes

Collective normally modifies abstract nouns in this meaning: memory, identity, behavior, interest, consciousness, experience. Do not confuse collective with collected. Collected can mean gathered or calm; collective means shared by a group or related to a group as a whole.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The community's collected memory was shaped by war. → ✅ The community's collective memory was shaped by war.
  • ❌ This is a collective problem for only one person. → ✅ This is an individual problem for one person.

TOEFL tip

When you see phrases like collective memory or collective behavior, expect the passage to discuss patterns shared by many people or animals, not private feelings or single actions.

Meaning 3 noun · countable /kəˈlektɪv/

A group of people, organizations, or workers who act together for a shared purpose, often with shared control or shared resources.

Natural examples

  1. A group of artists formed a collective to share studio space and equipment.
  2. The farmers' collective helped small farms sell their products directly to local markets.
  3. The workers ran the café as a collective, so major decisions were made together.
  4. A student collective organized free tutoring sessions for first-year students.
  5. The neighborhood food collective buys fresh produce from nearby farms.

Nuance & implication

As a noun, collective is more specific than group. It often suggests cooperation, shared ownership, shared decision-making, or a common social, artistic, political, or economic goal.

Grammar notes

As a noun, collective is countable: a collective, the collective, several collectives. Common patterns include a collective of artists, a workers' collective, a farming collective, and form a collective. Do not use it for every ordinary group; use it when members act together for a shared goal or structure.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ My family is a collective because we live together. → ✅ My family is a group, but not usually called a collective.
  • ❌ The collective of three random passengers waited for the bus. → ✅ The group of three passengers waited for the bus.

TOEFL tip

If collective is used after a, the, or another determiner, it may be a noun. In that case, it means a cooperative group, not just the idea of teamwork.

Word Family

collective

Part of speech: adjective / noun

Definition: shared by a group, or a group that works together for a shared purpose

  1. The class reached a collective decision.
  2. The artists formed a collective downtown.

collectively

Part of speech: adverb

Definition: together as a group; not separately

  1. The countries agreed to act collectively against the threat.
  2. The students collectively decided to ask for more time.

collect

Part of speech: verb

Definition: to bring things together, gather information, or receive money from people

  1. The researchers collected data from three different schools.
  2. The museum collects objects from the early twentieth century.

collection

Part of speech: noun

Definition: a group of things gathered together, often because they are similar or valuable

  1. The library has a large collection of historical maps.
  2. The study is based on a collection of interviews with teachers.

collector

Part of speech: noun

Definition: a person who gathers or keeps a particular type of object

  1. The painting was bought by a private collector.
  2. Stamp collectors often care about condition and rarity.

Common Collocations

collective action

Meaning: action taken by a group to reach a shared goal

Example: Reducing air pollution requires collective action from governments, businesses, and citizens.

collective effort

Meaning: work done together by many people

Example: The successful campaign was a collective effort by teachers, parents, and students.

collective responsibility

Meaning: responsibility shared by all members of a group

Example: The article argues that public health is a matter of collective responsibility.

collective decision

Meaning: a decision made by a group together

Example: The research team made a collective decision to change the design of the study.

collective decision-making

Meaning: the process of making decisions as a group

Example: Collective decision-making can improve trust, but it may also take more time.

collective memory

Meaning: shared memories or shared understanding of the past within a group

Example: Monuments can shape a nation's collective memory.

collective identity

Meaning: the shared sense of who a group is

Example: Music and language can strengthen a community's collective identity.

collective behavior

Meaning: behavior shown by many members of a group

Example: Scientists studied collective behavior in schools of fish.

collective interest

Meaning: the shared benefit or goal of a group

Example: The policy was designed to protect the collective interest of the community.

collective bargaining

Meaning: negotiation between workers as a group and an employer

Example: Collective bargaining helped workers negotiate better pay and safer conditions.

collective noun

Meaning: a noun that names a group, such as team, committee, or family

Example: Audience is a collective noun because it refers to a group of people.

workers' collective

Meaning: a workplace or organization controlled by workers together

Example: The workers' collective shared profits and made decisions democratically.

artist collective

Meaning: a group of artists who work together or share resources

Example: The artist collective opened a small gallery in an old warehouse.

farming collective

Meaning: a group of farmers who share land, tools, labor, or profits

Example: The farming collective allowed small farmers to buy equipment together.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

shared joint group communal cooperative

Shared is the simplest synonym when something belongs to several people. Joint often describes action or responsibility done together. Communal often suggests community life or shared resources. Cooperative emphasizes working together, especially for a common purpose.

Direct antonyms

individual personal private separate independent

Individual is the most common contrast when the focus is one person rather than the group. Personal and private contrast with shared or public group matters. Separate and independent emphasize not acting together.

Etymology

Collective comes from the same Latin root as collect, meaning to gather together. This history is useful because the modern word still keeps the idea of many people or things being brought together and treated as one group.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. The researchers will __________ data from more than 500 students.
  2. The project succeeded because of the team's __________ effort.
  3. The students worked __________ to design a fair solution.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The community showed collective responsibility after the flood.
  2. The team collective decided to change the plan.
  3. A collective of artists opened a shared studio downtown.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. Reducing plastic waste requires __________ from consumers, companies, and governments.
  2. The monument became part of the nation's __________.
  3. The union used __________ to negotiate higher wages.
Answer Key
  1. collect — The verb means to gather data or information.
  2. collective — The adjective describes an effort made by the whole team.
  3. collectively — The adverb means together as a group.
  4. Correct. Collective responsibility is a natural collocation.
  5. Incorrect. Better: The team collectively decided to change the plan or The team made a collective decision to change the plan.
  6. Correct. A collective of artists means a group of artists working together.
  7. collective action — This means action taken together by a group.
  8. collective memory — This means the shared memory or understanding of the past within a group.
  9. collective bargaining — This means workers negotiating as a group with an employer.
