🔹 Word: pioneer
🔊 Primary pronunciation: /ˌpaɪəˈnɪr/

🧠 Meaning 1
Part of speech: countable noun
Pronunciation: /ˌpaɪəˈnɪr/
Definition: a person who is among the first to develop a new idea, method, technology, or area of study

Examples:

1. The scientist was a pioneer in the study of human memory.
2. She is widely regarded as a pioneer of modern environmental economics.
3. Early pioneers in computer science created many of the basic ideas used today.

🧠 Meaning 2
Part of speech: countable noun
Pronunciation: /ˌpaɪəˈnɪr/
Definition: one of the first people to explore, travel to, or settle in a particular area

Examples:

1. The pioneers crossed the mountains in search of new farmland.
2. Life was difficult for the early pioneers who settled the region.
3. The museum displays tools used by pioneers during the westward expansion.

🧠 Meaning 3
Part of speech: transitive verb
Pronunciation: /ˌpaɪəˈnɪr/
Definition: to be among the first to develop, introduce, or use a new idea, method, technology, or activity

Examples:

1. The research team pioneered a new method for detecting the disease.
2. The company pioneered the use of recycled materials in its products.
3. These educators pioneered an approach that later spread worldwide.
