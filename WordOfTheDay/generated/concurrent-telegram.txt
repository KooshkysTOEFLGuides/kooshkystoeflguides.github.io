🔹 Word: concurrent
🔊 Primary pronunciation: /kənˈkɝːənt/

🧠 Meaning 1
Part of speech: adjective
Pronunciation: /kənˈkɝːənt/
Definition: happening, existing, or being done at the same time as something else

Examples:

1. The university scheduled three concurrent workshops, so students had to choose one.
2. The patient was treated for two concurrent infections.
3. The software can perform several concurrent tasks without slowing down.

🧠 Meaning 2
Part of speech: adjective
Pronunciation: /kənˈkɝːənt/
Definition: held or exercised at the same time by two or more authorities, organizations, or people

Examples:

1. Federal and state courts may have concurrent jurisdiction over certain cases.
2. The constitution gives the national and regional governments some concurrent powers.
3. Both departments have concurrent responsibility for protecting the site.
