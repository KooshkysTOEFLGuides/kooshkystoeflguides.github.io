🔹 Word: objective
🔊 Primary pronunciation: /əbˈdʒɛktɪv/

🧠 Meaning 1
Part of speech: adjective
Pronunciation: /əbˈdʒɛktɪv/
Definition: based on observable facts and evidence rather than personal opinions, feelings, or preferences

Examples:

1. Researchers should remain objective when interpreting their results.
2. The committee used objective criteria to evaluate every application.
3. Journalists are expected to provide objective accounts of important events.

🧠 Meaning 2
Part of speech: adjective
Pronunciation: /əbˈdʒɛktɪv/
Definition: existing outside a person's mind or able to be observed and measured independently

Examples:

1. The study combined patients' personal reports with objective measurements of sleep.
2. Scientists searched for objective evidence that the treatment was effective.
3. Income provides an objective measure of economic status, although it does not show every aspect of well-being.

🧠 Meaning 3
Part of speech: countable noun
Pronunciation: /əbˈdʒɛktɪv/
Definition: a specific result that a person or organization intends to achieve

Examples:

1. The primary objective of the program is to improve access to higher education.
2. The research team achieved its main objective ahead of schedule.
3. Before beginning the project, the students established several clear objectives.
