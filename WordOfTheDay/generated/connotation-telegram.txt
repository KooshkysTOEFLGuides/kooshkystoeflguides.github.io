🔹 Word: connotation
🔊 Primary pronunciation: /ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃən/

🧠 Meaning 1
Part of speech: countable noun
Pronunciation: /ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃən/
Definition: an idea or feeling that a word suggests in addition to its direct meaning

Examples:

1. The word home often has connotations of comfort, safety, and belonging.
2. Although slim and skinny have similar meanings, skinny often has a more negative connotation.
3. In many cultures, the color white has connotations of purity and peace.

🧠 Meaning 2
Part of speech: countable noun
Pronunciation: /ˌkɑːnəˈteɪʃən/
Definition: an additional idea, attitude, or implication associated with an object, action, situation, or form of behavior

Examples:

1. For some employees, working from home has connotations of freedom and flexibility.
2. The building's grand design carries connotations of power and authority.
3. Refusing the invitation might have unintended social connotations.
