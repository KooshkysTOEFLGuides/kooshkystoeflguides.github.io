🔹 Word: clairvoyant
🔊 Primary pronunciation: /klɛrˈvɔɪənt/

🧠 Meaning 1
Part of speech: adjective
Pronunciation: /klɛrˈvɔɪənt/
Definition: believed to be able to know about future, distant, or hidden events without receiving information through the ordinary senses

Examples:

1. She claimed to be clairvoyant and able to predict major events.
2. The story features a clairvoyant woman who sees visions of the future.
3. I am not clairvoyant, so you need to tell me what you are thinking.

🧠 Meaning 2
Part of speech: adjective
Pronunciation: /klɛrˈvɔɪənt/
Definition: showing an unusually accurate understanding of what will happen or of something that is not yet obvious

Examples:

1. His warning about the economic crisis now seems almost clairvoyant.
2. The author's clairvoyant analysis anticipated many later technological changes.
3. Her prediction that remote work would become widespread appeared remarkably clairvoyant.

🧠 Meaning 3
Part of speech: countable noun
Pronunciation: /klɛrˈvɔɪənt/
Definition: a person who claims or is believed to know about future, distant, or hidden events through a supernatural ability

Examples:

1. The clairvoyant claimed that she could describe events occurring far away.
2. He visited a clairvoyant who offered to predict his future.
3. The experiment tested whether self-described clairvoyants could identify hidden objects.
