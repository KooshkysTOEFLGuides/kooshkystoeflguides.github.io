Meanings and usage

The core definition and examples are always included. Additional notes appear only when they are accurate and useful.

Meaning 1

Quality or feature

countable noun /ˈætrəˌbjuːt/ neutral to formal; common in academic, professional, scientific, and technical writing
🔊 Hear countable noun pronunciation

Pronunciation: /ˈætrəˌbjuːt/ in the phrase “an attribute.” This word has another pronunciation in at least one other meaning.

a quality or feature that is considered a typical or important part of a person, thing, or system

In simpler words: an important quality or feature

Typical pattern: an attribute of + person, object, or system

Natural examples

  1. Patience is an important attribute of an effective teacher.
  2. Flexibility is one of the material's most useful attributes.
  3. The researchers examined several attributes of successful organizations.
  4. Physical strength was once regarded as an essential attribute of military leadership.
  5. The ability to adapt is a valuable attribute in a rapidly changing workplace.
  6. Each plant species has attributes that help it survive in a particular environment.
  7. Accuracy and clarity are desirable attributes in academic writing.
  8. The software allows users to search for products according to specific attributes.

Nuance and implication

Central nuance: An attribute is a quality that belongs to or helps describe a person, object, system, or category.

Usual implication: The quality is often considered characteristic, important, or useful for identifying or evaluating something.

Compared with “possession”: A possession is something that a person or organization owns. An attribute is a quality or feature that something has.

Grammar notes

How to use it
Use an attribute for one quality and attributes for several qualities.
What can follow it
It is often followed by of and the person, object, or system that has the quality.
Common preposition
Use an attribute of something, as in an important attribute of good leadership.
Noun use
This is a countable noun, so the singular normally needs a, an, or the.

Useful patterns

an attribute of + person, thing, or system

Use this to identify something that possesses a particular quality.

Example: Reliability is a central attribute of a well-designed transportation system.

a desirable or valuable attribute

Use this for a quality that is considered useful or positive.

Example: Curiosity is a valuable attribute in scientific research.

a defining attribute

Use this for a quality that strongly distinguishes something from other things.

Example: The ability to regulate body temperature is a defining attribute of mammals.

possess or have an attribute

Use this when stating that a person or thing has a particular quality.

Example: The material possesses several attributes that make it suitable for construction.

Common wrong usage

Wrong: Patience is an attribute for a good teacher.

Correct: Patience is an attribute of a good teacher.

Use an attribute of someone or something.

Wrong: Honesty is important attribute in a leader.

Correct: Honesty is an important attribute in a leader.

The singular countable noun attribute needs an article.

Wrong: The machine has many useful attributions.

Correct: The machine has many useful attributes.

Attributes are qualities or features. Attributions are explanations of causes or statements about who created something.

Meaning 2

Regard as caused by

transitive verb /əˈtrɪbjuːt/ formal; very common in academic, scientific, historical, medical, and professional writing
🔊 Hear transitive verb pronunciation

Pronunciation: /əˈtrɪbjuːt/ in the phrase “attribute the change.” This word has another pronunciation in at least one other meaning.

to believe that an event, condition, or result was caused by a particular person, thing, or factor

In simpler words: believe that something caused a result

Typical pattern: attribute + result or condition + to + cause

Natural examples

  1. Scientists attribute the decline in the bird population to habitat loss.
  2. She attributed her success to careful preparation and support from her colleagues.
  3. The improvement cannot be attributed entirely to the new policy.
  4. Researchers attributed the difference between the groups to variations in diet.
  5. Some historians attribute the collapse of the society to a prolonged drought.
  6. The company attributed the delay to unexpected technical problems.
  7. Doctors initially attributed his symptoms to stress.
  8. Analysts attribute much of the recent economic growth to increased consumer spending.

Nuance and implication

Central nuance: The verb identifies what someone believes to be the cause or explanation of a result.

Usual implication: The connection may be supported by evidence, but attribute does not necessarily mean that the cause has been proved with complete certainty.

Compared with “prove”: To attribute a result to something is to identify it as the believed cause. To prove that it caused the result requires stronger and more conclusive evidence.

Grammar notes

How to use it
Place the result, event, or condition directly after attribute.
What can follow it
After the result, use to and then the believed cause.
Common preposition
Use attribute something to something, as in attribute the decline to pollution.
Position in a sentence
The passive form is very common: The decline was attributed to pollution.

Useful patterns

attribute + result + to + cause

Use this when identifying the believed cause of an event or condition.

Example: Researchers attributed the increase in crop production to improved irrigation.

be attributed to + cause

Use the passive form when the result is more important than the person explaining it.

Example: The population decline was attributed to disease and food shortages.

attribute success or failure to + factor

Use this when explaining why a person, project, or organization succeeded or failed.

Example: The team attributed its success to careful planning.

largely, partly, or mainly attribute something to + cause

Use these words to show how much of the result is explained by a particular factor.

Example: Economists largely attribute the recovery to increased investment.

Common wrong usage

Wrong: Researchers attribute the decline from climate change.

Correct: Researchers attribute the decline to climate change.

Use attribute something to a cause.

Wrong: The company attributed to poor planning the failure.

Correct: The company attributed the failure to poor planning.

The usual order is attribute the result to the cause.

Wrong: The improvement was attributed by the new treatment.

Correct: The improvement was attributed to the new treatment.

Use to before the believed cause. By would identify the person making the attribution.

Wrong: Scientists attributed that pollution caused the decline.

Correct: Scientists attributed the decline to pollution.

Use attribute a result to a cause rather than attribute that followed by a clause.

Meaning 3

Regard as created or said by

transitive verb /əˈtrɪbjuːt/ formal; common in history, art, literature, science, and academic writing
🔊 Hear transitive verb pronunciation

Pronunciation: /əˈtrɪbjuːt/ in the phrase “attribute the painting.” This word has another pronunciation in at least one other meaning.

to believe or state that a work, statement, idea, or achievement was created, said, or accomplished by a particular person or group

In simpler words: believe that someone created or said something

Typical pattern: attribute + work, quotation, discovery, or idea + to + person or group

Natural examples

  1. The painting was originally attributed to a famous Italian artist.
  2. This quotation is often attributed to Einstein, although he may never have said it.
  3. Historians attribute the invention to several different researchers.
  4. The poem was wrongly attributed to a writer who lived a century later.
  5. Scholars have attributed the ancient manuscript to an unknown religious community.
  6. The discovery is generally attributed to a team of French scientists.
  7. Several architectural innovations are attributed to the Roman engineer.
  8. The theory was once attributed to Darwin but was actually proposed by an earlier scholar.

Nuance and implication

Central nuance: The verb connects a work, statement, idea, or achievement with the person believed to be responsible for it.

Usual implication: The identification may be accepted, uncertain, disputed, or later shown to be incorrect.

Compared with “credit”: To credit someone with something usually emphasizes recognizing their contribution or achievement. To attribute something to someone focuses on identifying them as its source, creator, or author.

Grammar notes

How to use it
Place the work, statement, idea, or achievement directly after attribute.
What can follow it
Use to and then the person, group, culture, or period believed to be responsible.
Common preposition
Use attribute something to someone, as in attribute the poem to an unknown writer.
Position in a sentence
The passive form is especially common: The quotation is attributed to Mark Twain.

Useful patterns

attribute + work or statement + to + person

Use this when identifying the believed author, speaker, or creator.

Example: Experts attributed the sculpture to a local artist.

be attributed to + person or group

Use the passive form when the work or achievement is the main focus.

Example: The discovery is usually attributed to two independent research teams.

wrongly or mistakenly attribute something to + person

Use this when the accepted source or creator is incorrect.

Example: The quotation is frequently misattributed to Abraham Lincoln.

generally or traditionally attribute something to + person

Use this when an attribution is widely accepted but may not be completely certain.

Example: The epic poem is traditionally attributed to Homer.

Common wrong usage

Wrong: The quotation is attributed from a famous scientist.

Correct: The quotation is attributed to a famous scientist.

Use attribute something to a person.

Wrong: Historians attribute the scientist for the discovery.

Correct: Historians attribute the discovery to the scientist.

Place the discovery after attribute and the person after to.

Wrong: The painting was attributed as Leonardo da Vinci.

Correct: The painting was attributed to Leonardo da Vinci.

Use to, not as, before the believed creator.

Morphology

Word family

Only common and educationally useful family members are included.

countable or uncountable noun

attribution

/ˌætrəˈbjuːʃən/

the act of explaining something as the result of a particular cause, or of identifying someone as the creator of a work or idea

Relationship: This noun names the act or result of attributing a cause, work, statement, or achievement.

Pattern: the attribution of + result + to + cause or the attribution of + work + to + creator

Usage note: It can be countable when referring to a particular explanation or identification and uncountable when referring to the general practice.

  1. The attribution of the decline to climate change remains controversial.
  2. Experts questioned the painting's attribution to the famous artist.
  3. Accurate attribution is essential when using another person's ideas.

adjective

attributable

/əˈtrɪbjətəbəl/

considered to have been caused by a particular person, thing, or factor

Relationship: This adjective describes a result that can be attributed to a particular cause.

Pattern: be attributable to + cause

Usage note: It is formal and especially common in academic, legal, scientific, and financial writing.

  1. Most of the improvement was attributable to better medical care.
  2. The damage attributable to the storm was less severe than expected.

Meaning relations

Synonyms and antonyms

Only clear and useful relationships are included.

Close synonyms

characteristic

countable noun · Quality or feature

Shared meaning: Both words can name a quality or feature of a person or thing.

Difference: Characteristic is a common general word for a typical quality. Attribute is slightly more formal and may refer to any important or identifiable quality, whether or not it is typical.

Example: Thick fur is a characteristic of many animals that live in cold climates.

trait

countable noun · Quality or feature

Shared meaning: Both words can refer to a quality that helps describe someone or something.

Difference: Trait is especially common for qualities of a person's character or inherited features of an organism. Attribute can describe people, objects, systems, and abstract concepts.

Example: Curiosity is a valuable personality trait.

ascribe

transitive verb · all senses

Shared meaning: Both verbs can connect a result with a cause or a work with its believed creator.

Difference: Ascribe is a close formal synonym of the verb attribute. Attribute is more common, especially in academic and scientific writing.

Example: The researchers ascribed the improvement to changes in diet.

credit

transitive verb · Regard as created or said by

Shared meaning: Both verbs can identify someone as responsible for an achievement or idea.

Difference: Credit often emphasizes giving recognition or praise for an achievement. Attribute focuses more neutrally on identifying the believed source or creator.

Example: The team credited its coach with developing the successful strategy.

Precision

Words learners may confuse

contribute

transitive verb versus intransitive or transitive verb

Why it is confusing: Both words frequently appear in explanations of causes, but the direction of the relationship is different.

Key difference: Attribute means connect a result with its believed cause. Contribute means help cause a result or give something toward a shared effort.

attribute: Researchers attribute the decline to habitat destruction.

contribute: Habitat destruction contributed to the decline.

attribution

noun or verb versus noun

Why it is confusing: The words share the same root but perform different grammatical jobs.

Key difference: Attribute can be a noun meaning a quality or a verb meaning connect something with a cause or creator. Attribution is the noun for the act or result of making that connection.

attribute: Flexibility is a useful attribute of the material.

attribution: The attribution of the painting to that artist remains uncertain.
