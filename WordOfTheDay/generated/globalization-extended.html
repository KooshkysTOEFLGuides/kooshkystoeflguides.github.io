Dictionary guide

Meanings and usage

The core definition and examples are always included. Additional notes appear only when they are accurate and useful.

Meaning 1

Growing worldwide interconnectedness

uncountable noun /ˌɡloʊbələˈzeɪʃən/ neutral to formal; very common in economics, politics, sociology, business, history, and environmental studies
🔊 Hear uncountable noun pronunciation

the process through which countries, economies, cultures, and people become increasingly connected and dependent on one another around the world

In simpler words: the world becoming more connected

Typical pattern: globalization of + industry, trade, culture, or economy

Natural examples

  1. Globalization has allowed companies to sell their products in markets around the world.
  2. Advances in transportation and communication have accelerated globalization.
  3. Critics argue that globalization can weaken local industries and traditions.
  4. The course examines how globalization has influenced employment and migration.
  5. Globalization has made it easier for ideas and cultural products to cross national borders.
  6. Some developing countries have benefited from globalization through increased trade and investment.
  7. The environmental effects of globalization extend far beyond individual national borders.

Nuance and implication

Central nuance: The word describes a broad process involving the movement of goods, money, information, technology, people, and cultural influences across national borders.

Usual implication: Globalization may create opportunities and greater cooperation, but it may also increase inequality, economic dependence, cultural pressure, or environmental harm.

Compared with “internationalization”: Internationalization often describes a deliberate effort by a company, university, or organization to operate in multiple countries. Globalization is the broader process through which the world as a whole becomes increasingly interconnected.

Grammar notes

How to use it
Use globalization as the name of a general process.
What can follow it
It is often followed by of and a field or activity, as in the globalization of trade. It can also be followed by verbs such as increase, accelerate, transform, affect, or contribute.
Common preposition
Use the globalization of something when identifying the area becoming more global, as in the globalization of higher education.
Noun use
Globalization is normally uncountable, so do not use a or an before it. The form globalizations is extremely rare and is not normally needed by learners.
Position in a sentence
It can appear without the when discussing the process generally. Use the when referring to a specific form or aspect, as in the globalization of financial markets.

Useful patterns

the globalization of + noun

Use this to identify the industry, activity, institution, or cultural area becoming more internationally connected.

Example: The globalization of food production has created complex international supply chains.

globalization has led to + noun

Use this to describe a result of growing international interconnectedness.

Example: Globalization has led to greater competition between manufacturers.

globalization has made it easier to + verb

Use this to explain an activity that has become easier because national borders matter less.

Example: Globalization has made it easier to exchange scientific knowledge.

the effects or impact of globalization

Use this when discussing the consequences of globalization.

Example: The study investigates the impact of globalization on small farming communities.

accelerate or drive globalization

Use this for a force that causes countries and societies to become connected more rapidly.

Example: Digital technology has helped drive globalization.

Common wrong usage

Wrong: The globalization has changed modern business.

Correct: Globalization has changed modern business.

Do not normally use the when discussing globalization as a general process.

Wrong: A globalization has created new economic opportunities.

Correct: Globalization has created new economic opportunities.

Globalization is normally uncountable, so it is not used with a.

Wrong: Globalization between trade has increased rapidly.

Correct: The globalization of trade has increased rapidly.

Use the globalization of something, not globalization between something.

Wrong: Globalization only means that companies sell products abroad.

Correct: Globalization includes international trade as well as growing cultural, technological, political, and social connections.

The meaning is broader than business or trade alone.

Word family

adjective

global

/ˈɡloʊbəl/

relating to the whole world or involving many parts of the world

Relationship: This adjective describes something involving or affecting the world as a whole.

Pattern: global + economy, market, problem, network, trade, or population

  1. Climate change is a global problem that requires international cooperation.
  2. The company has developed a global network of suppliers.

adverb

globally

/ˈɡloʊbəli/

throughout the world or in a way that involves the whole world

Relationship: This adverb describes something happening or existing worldwide.

Pattern: operate, compete, spread, or be recognized globally

  1. The disease affects millions of people globally.
  2. The organization operates both locally and globally.

transitive verb

globalize

/ˈɡloʊbəˌlaɪz/

to make a business, industry, activity, or system operate or develop throughout the world

Relationship: This verb describes causing something to become more global.

Pattern: globalize + industry, market, business, production, or culture

Usage note: The verb is less common than globalization and is mainly used in formal economic and business contexts.

  1. The company plans to globalize its production network.
  2. Digital platforms have helped globalize the entertainment industry.

Meaning relations

Synonyms and antonyms

Only clear and useful relationships are included.

Close synonyms

global integration

uncountable noun phrase · Growing worldwide interconnectedness

Shared meaning: Both expressions describe increasing connections between countries and societies.

Difference: Global integration emphasizes different economies, systems, or societies becoming connected parts of a larger whole. Globalization is broader and can also include the spread of culture, information, technology, and social practices.

Example: Regional trade agreements have encouraged global integration.

internationalization

uncountable noun · Growing worldwide interconnectedness

Shared meaning: Both involve increasing activity and connections across national borders.

Difference: Internationalization often describes a deliberate effort by a company, university, or institution to expand its international activities. Globalization usually refers to the wider historical process affecting the world as a whole.

Example: The university's internationalization strategy includes recruiting more overseas students.

Direct or useful antonyms

deglobalization

uncountable noun · Growing worldwide interconnectedness

Opposition: Deglobalization is the process through which international economic, political, or social connections become weaker or less extensive.

Usage note: The word is especially common in economics and political analysis.

Example: Trade restrictions may contribute to deglobalization.

Precision

Words learners may confuse

globalism

uncountable noun

Why it is confusing: Both words relate to global connections, but one names a process while the other often names an idea or policy.

Key difference: Globalization is the process through which the world becomes more interconnected. Globalism is a belief, policy, or way of thinking that supports organizing activities and cooperation at a global level.

globalization: Globalization has increased the international movement of goods and information.

globalism: The politician criticized globalism and called for greater national independence.

internationalization

uncountable noun

Why it is confusing: The two processes both involve activity across national borders and may occur together.

Key difference: Globalization is a broad worldwide process, while internationalization often refers to a specific organization deliberately expanding its international operations.

globalization: Globalization has transformed patterns of international trade.

internationalization: The internationalization of the university included creating exchange programs.
