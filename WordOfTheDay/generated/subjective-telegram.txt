🔹 Word: subjective
🔊 Primary pronunciation: /səbˈdʒɛktɪv/

🧠 Meaning 1
Part of speech: adjective
Pronunciation: /səbˈdʒɛktɪv/
Definition: based on a person's opinions, feelings, or preferences rather than only on facts that can be measured or proved

Examples:

1. Judgments about artistic quality are often highly subjective.
2. The interview process was criticized because the final decisions seemed too subjective.
3. Whether a building is beautiful is partly a subjective question.

🧠 Meaning 2
Part of speech: adjective
Pronunciation: /səbˈdʒɛktɪv/
Definition: experienced, perceived, or understood within an individual person's mind and therefore not fully observable or measurable by others

Examples:

1. Pain is a subjective experience that can be difficult to measure precisely.
2. The survey asked participants to report their subjective sense of well-being.
3. People may give different descriptions of the same event because perception is subjective.
