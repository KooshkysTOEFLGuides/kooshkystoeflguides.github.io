🔹 Word: democratize
🔊 Primary pronunciation: /dɪˈmɑːkrəˌtaɪz/

🧠 Meaning 1
Part of speech: transitive verb
Pronunciation: /dɪˈmɑːkrəˌtaɪz/
Definition: to make a country, organization, or system more democratic by giving people more power or a greater role in decisions

Examples:

1. The reforms were intended to democratize the country's political system.
2. Student representatives argued that the university should democratize its decision-making process.
3. The new constitution helped democratize several public institutions.

🧠 Meaning 2
Part of speech: transitive verb
Pronunciation: /dɪˈmɑːkrəˌtaɪz/
Definition: to make information, education, technology, or another useful resource available to many more people instead of only a small or powerful group

Examples:

1. Online libraries can democratize access to academic research.
2. Low-cost smartphones have helped democratize access to digital services.
3. The museum hopes to democratize art by offering free virtual exhibitions.
