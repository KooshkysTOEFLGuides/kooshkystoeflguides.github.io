🔹 Word: echelon
🔊 Primary pronunciation: /ˈɛʃəˌlɑːn/

🧠 Meaning 1
Part of speech: countable noun
Pronunciation: /ˈɛʃəˌlɑːn/
Definition: a particular level of authority, importance, or status within an organization, profession, or society

Examples:

1. The proposal must be approved by the upper echelons of management.
2. Women remain underrepresented in the highest echelons of the industry.
3. Information often moves slowly between different echelons of a large organization.

🧠 Meaning 2
Part of speech: countable noun
Pronunciation: /ˈɛʃəˌlɑːn/
Definition: a military formation in which each group of soldiers, vehicles, ships, or aircraft is positioned behind and to one side of the group ahead

Examples:

1. The aircraft approached the target in an echelon formation.
2. The ships moved in echelon to maintain a clear view of one another.
3. The soldiers advanced in echelon across the open ground.
