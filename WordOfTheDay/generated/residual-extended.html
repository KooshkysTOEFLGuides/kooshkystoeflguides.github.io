Meanings and usage

The core definition and examples are always included. Additional notes appear only when they are accurate and useful.

Meaning 1

Remaining after a process

adjective /rɪˈzɪdʒuəl/ formal; common in science, medicine, economics, engineering, and academic writing
🔊 Hear adjective pronunciation

remaining after most of something has disappeared, been removed, or ended

In simpler words: left over after most is gone

Typical pattern: residual + effect, amount, heat, moisture, chemical, symptom, or risk

Natural examples

  1. The treatment reduced the infection, but some residual symptoms remained.
  2. Residual heat from the machinery continued to warm the room.
  3. The soil contained residual chemicals from earlier farming practices.
  4. Researchers found residual traces of the substance in the water.
  5. The conflict left residual tension between the two communities.
  6. Residual moisture can damage the equipment if it is not removed.
  7. The economic crisis had residual effects that continued for several years.

Nuance and implication

Central nuance: The word emphasizes that the main part has gone or been dealt with, but a smaller part still remains.

Usual implication: The remaining part may continue to have an effect even though it is relatively small.

Compared with “remaining”: Remaining is a general word for anything left. Residual is more formal and often suggests that something is left after a specific process, treatment, calculation, or event.

Grammar notes

How to use it
Use it before a noun to describe something that remains after a process or event.
What can follow it
It commonly comes before nouns such as effect, amount, heat, moisture, chemical, symptom, damage, risk, and value.
Position in a sentence
It is most often used before a noun, as in residual damage. Use remaining rather than residual in many ordinary everyday situations.

Useful patterns

residual + effect or impact

Use this for an effect that continues after its main cause or event has ended.

Example: The policy had residual effects on employment in the region.

residual + substance or material

Use this for a small amount of material left after cleaning, processing, or use.

Example: Residual oil was found inside the pipe.

residual + symptom or damage

Use this for a problem that remains after recovery or repair.

Example: Most patients recovered fully, although a few experienced residual pain.

residual + value

Use this for the estimated value that an asset still has after use or depreciation.

Example: The vehicle is expected to have a high residual value after three years.

Common wrong usage

Wrong: There was a residual of tension after the meeting.

Correct: There was residual tension after the meeting.

When residual describes a noun, place it directly before that noun.

Wrong: The medicine caused some remained effects.

Correct: The medicine caused some residual effects.

Use residual, not remained, as an adjective before a noun.

Wrong: I ate the residual pizza for lunch.

Correct: I ate the leftover pizza for lunch.

Residual is generally too formal and technical for ordinary food left after a meal. Leftover is more natural.

Meaning 2

Amount left over

countable noun /rɪˈzɪdʒuəl/ formal and technical; used mainly in science, finance, mathematics, and manufacturing
🔊 Hear countable noun pronunciation

an amount that remains after other amounts have been removed, used, or calculated

In simpler words: an amount left after the rest is removed

Typical pattern: a residual from + process or the residual after + calculation

Natural examples

  1. The researchers examined the residuals left after the chemical reaction.
  2. Any residual from the manufacturing process must be disposed of safely.
  3. The company receives the residual after all operating expenses have been paid.
  4. The filtering system removes most particles, but a small residual may remain.
  5. The accountant calculated the residual after subtracting the fixed costs.
  6. Scientists analyzed the residuals to identify materials that had not broken down.
  7. The residual was too small to affect the final result.

Nuance and implication

Central nuance: As a noun, residual refers to the actual amount or quantity that is left, not merely its quality of remaining.

Usual implication: The amount is often determined after subtraction, processing, filtering, or distribution.

Grammar notes

How to use it
Use a residual for one remaining amount and residuals for several separate remaining amounts.
What can follow it
It may be followed by from to identify the process that produced it, or after to identify the calculation or deduction.
Common preposition
Say a residual from the process or the residual after expenses were paid.
Noun use
This is countable in technical use. However, residue is often more natural when referring to physical material left on a surface.

Useful patterns

a residual from + process

Use this for an amount left as a result of a process.

Example: The laboratory tested a residual from the purification process.

the residual after + deduction or payment

Use this for the amount remaining after costs or other amounts have been subtracted.

Example: Investors receive the residual after all debts have been paid.

calculate the residual

Use this when the remaining amount is found mathematically.

Example: The analyst calculated the residual by subtracting total costs from revenue.

Common wrong usage

Wrong: The workers cleaned the residual from the table.

Correct: The workers cleaned the residue from the table.

Residue is normally used for physical material left on a surface. Residual as a noun is more technical and often refers to a calculated or measured amount.

Wrong: The company kept residual after paying its debts.

Correct: The company kept the residual after paying its debts.

A singular countable use normally needs a, an, or the.

Meaning 3

Difference from a prediction

countable noun /rɪˈzɪdʒuəl/ technical; common in statistics, data analysis, economics, and scientific research
🔊 Hear countable noun pronunciation

in statistics, the difference between an observed value and the value predicted by a model

In simpler words: the gap between an actual and predicted value

Typical pattern: observed value minus predicted value equals residual

Natural examples

  1. A large residual may indicate that the model does not explain the observation well.
  2. The students calculated a residual for each data point.
  3. The residuals were plotted to check whether the statistical model was appropriate.
  4. A positive residual means that the observed value is higher than the predicted value.
  5. Several unusually large residuals suggested that the data contained outliers.
  6. The model was revised to reduce the size of the residuals.
  7. Randomly distributed residuals generally provide evidence that the model fits the data reasonably well.

Nuance and implication

Central nuance: A residual measures how far one observation is from the value that a statistical model predicts.

Usual implication: Small and randomly distributed residuals often suggest a better-fitting model, while large or patterned residuals may reveal problems.

Grammar notes

How to use it
Use a residual for the difference associated with one observation and residuals for the differences across a data set.
What can follow it
It often appears with verbs such as calculate, examine, analyze, plot, minimize, and reduce.
Noun use
This is a countable technical noun. The plural residuals is especially common when discussing an entire data set.

Useful patterns

calculate a residual

Use this for finding the difference between one observed value and its predicted value.

Example: Calculate a residual for each point in the data set.

analyze or plot the residuals

Use this when examining whether a statistical model fits the data well.

Example: The researchers plotted the residuals before accepting the model.

a positive or negative residual

Use positive when the observation is above the prediction and negative when it is below.

Example: The final observation produced a large negative residual.

Common wrong usage

Wrong: The residual is the predicted value itself.

Correct: The residual is the difference between the observed value and the predicted value.

A residual is a difference, not the predicted value.

Wrong: The researcher calculated the residuals between the two groups.

Correct: The researcher calculated the residuals for the observations.

Statistical residuals are normally calculated for observations by comparing observed and predicted values.

Word family

countable or uncountable noun

residue

/ˈrɛzəˌduː/

a substance or small amount of material that remains after a process such as burning, evaporation, or cleaning

Relationship: Residue names material that remains, while residual commonly describes something that remains.

Pattern: residue on + surface or residue from + process

Usage note: Use residue without a number for material in general and residues for separate types or deposits.

  1. The cleaner left a sticky residue on the glass.
  2. Scientists tested the soil for pesticide residues.

adverb

residually

/rɪˈzɪdʒuəli/

in a way that remains after the main part has been removed, completed, or explained

Relationship: This adverb describes something occurring or existing as a residual.

Usage note: It is uncommon and mainly appears in specialized academic or technical writing.

  1. The substance was residually present in the soil.
  2. The remaining funds were residually allocated among the partners.

Meaning relations

Synonyms and antonyms

Only clear and useful relationships are included.

Close synonyms

remaining

adjective · Remaining after a process

Shared meaning: Both words describe something that is still left.

Difference: Remaining is more general and common in everyday language. Residual is more formal and often refers to something left after a particular process or event.

Example: The remaining funds will be used for maintenance.

leftover

adjective · Remaining after a process

Shared meaning: Both can describe something that remains after the main part has been used.

Difference: Leftover is more informal and is especially common for food, materials, or unused items. Residual is more technical and academic.

Example: The leftover materials were stored for future use.

remainder

countable noun · Amount left over

Shared meaning: Both words can refer to an amount left after subtraction or use.

Difference: Remainder is a common general word for what is left. Residual is more formal and often refers to an amount produced by a calculation or technical process.

Example: The remainder of the budget was transferred to the next year.

error

countable noun · Difference from a prediction

Shared meaning: Both can describe a difference between a value and a reference point.

Difference: In statistics, error may refer to the difference between an observed value and the true underlying value, which may be unknown. A residual is the observable difference between an observed value and a model's predicted value.

Example: Measurement error can reduce the accuracy of an experiment.

Precision

Words learners may confuse

residue

adjective versus noun

Why it is confusing: The words share the same root and both involve the idea of something being left behind.

Key difference: Residual is usually an adjective meaning remaining, while residue is usually a noun referring to physical material left after a process.

residual: The laboratory detected residual chemicals in the water.

residue: A white residue remained at the bottom of the container.
