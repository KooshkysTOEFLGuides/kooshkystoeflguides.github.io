==================================================
GENERAL LEXICOGRAPHIC RULES
==================================================

Use American English unless a British/American difference matters.

Include all common TOEFL-relevant meanings of the word.

Do NOT include archaic, extremely rare, slangy, or overly technical meanings unless TOEFL students are genuinely likely to encounter them.

Definitions must use B1 to mid-B2 language without removing important nuance.

Examples must be natural, idiomatic, varied, well-contexted, and meaning-specific. Avoid empty examples such as “This is important.”

Give accurate American English IPA. Do not provide audio URLs, audio filenames, SSML, phoneme markup, or instructions for a TTS system. Local scripts create the audio files.

Be accurate about:

* part of speech
* whether a verb needs an object
* prepositions
* whether a noun is normally used with a/an, in the plural, or without a number
* adjective, noun, and verb patterns
* words and structures that naturally follow the headword
* established collocations
* register and frequency
* meaning implications
* semantic and sentence-structure mistakes

Do NOT invent strange or rare collocations.

Do NOT treat approximate synonyms as fully interchangeable. When you include a synonym, explain the difference.

Do NOT force an antonym, confusable, word-family member, grammar note, contrast word, etymology detail, or exercise type merely to make the page look complete.

Do NOT include ordinary plural forms or ordinary verb inflections in the word-family section unless they are irregular or educationally important.

Do NOT overuse emojis. Use them only in the Telegram post.

Perform a final accuracy check before answering.

==================================================
PRONUNCIATION AND AUDIO-PHRASE RULES
==================================================

The local Edge-TTS script generates audio after the HTML files are created.

For an ordinary word whose pronunciation does not change across meanings:

* Give pronunciation.primary_ipa.
* Give the correct ipa inside every meaning.
* Normally omit pronunciation.audio_phrase.
* Normally omit audio_phrase inside meanings.
* The generator will use the isolated headword as the spoken text and audio filename.

For a heteronym or any word whose pronunciation changes across meanings or parts of speech:

* Give the correct ipa separately inside every meaning.
* Include audio_phrase inside EVERY meaning.
* Each audio_phrase must be a short, natural phrase that contains the exact headword and makes the intended pronunciation clear.
* Prefer one to five words.
* Use contextual phrases such as “a record” for the noun and “to record” for the verb.
* Use a different phrase for each different pronunciation.
* Do not use a full explanatory sentence merely to force the pronunciation.
* pronunciation.audio_phrase is optional because the generator can use the first meaning’s phrase as the primary button.

The phrase is content, not formatting. Do not add quotation marks around it inside JSON.

==================================================
LEARNER-FRIENDLY GRAMMAR POLICY
==================================================

Grammar notes are optional and must be understandable to B1–B2 learners.

Write grammar notes as practical usage advice, not as a linguistic analysis.

Prefer wording such as:

* “Use it before a noun.”
* “It is usually followed by to and then a noun or an -ing form.”
* “This verb normally needs a person or thing after it.”
* “In this meaning, we usually say a notion, but notions is also possible.”

Avoid unexplained technical labels such as:

* to-infinitive
* infinitival complement
* gerund-participial clause
* complementation
* predicative adjective
* attributive adjective
* ditransitive
* clausal object

When a technical term is genuinely necessary, explain it immediately in ordinary language and give a short example.

Patterns must be visible learner-friendly forms such as prone to + noun or prone to + -ing form. Do not write abstract grammar formulas that students cannot interpret.

==================================================
ACCURACY-FIRST OMISSION POLICY
==================================================

The JSON contract intentionally has a small required core and many optional fields.

Accuracy is more important than filling every possible field.

REQUIRED CORE

The JSON must include only these top-level keys as a minimum:

* document_type
* schema_version
* slug
* word
* date
* pronunciation
* meanings

For each meaning, these fields are required:

* sense_title
* part_of_speech
* ipa
* definition
* examples

Each meaning must contain at least five natural examples because examples are core learning content.

The meaning-level audio_phrase is conditionally required only when different meanings have different IPA pronunciations.

OPTIONAL CONTENT

All other top-level sections and most detailed subfields are optional. Include an optional key only when it adds reliable, useful, TOEFL-relevant information.

When useful and confidently supported, aim to include substantial detail such as:

* a tailored hero area
* plain-English equivalents
* register information
* nuance and implications
* simple, learner-friendly grammar guidance
* useful surface patterns
* genuine common errors
* common word-family members
* established collocations
* close synonyms with differences
* genuine antonyms
* genuinely confusable words
* carefully documented etymology
* suitable practice exercises

However:

* Omit an optional top-level key entirely when there is no strong content for it.
* Inside an optional object, include only the subfields you can support accurately.
* Do not write filler such as “Not applicable,” “No important information,” “Unknown,” or “There is no...” merely to occupy a field.
* Do not use an empty string, empty object, empty array, null, or a guessed statement as a substitute for omission.
* Etymology is entirely optional. If only the origin is confidently known, include only etymology.origin. Do not reconstruct uncertain historical stages.
* A contrast term inside nuance is optional. Include contrast_term and contrast_explanation together, or omit both.
* Grammar notes should match the part of speech and should explain real learner choices in ordinary language.
* Omit word_family when there are no common educationally useful derivatives.
* Omit antonyms rather than giving a context-dependent or misleading opposite.
* Omit exercises.word_family_choice when the word has no suitable family contrast.
* Omit exercises.collocation_practice when the page does not contain reliable collocations.

The final page should be as detailed as the evidence supports—not artificially uniform from word to word.
