Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 transitive verb /ˈæləˌkeɪt/

To officially give or set aside money, time, space, people, or other resources for a particular purpose.

Natural examples

  1. The government allocated more funding to public schools.
  2. We need to allocate at least two hours for the TOEFL practice test.
  3. The university allocated additional space for student research projects.
  4. The company allocated extra staff to the customer service department.
  5. Several million dollars were allocated for environmental protection.

Nuance & implication

Allocate sounds planned, official, and practical. It usually implies that resources are limited, so someone must decide where they should go. It is more formal and academic than simply saying give.

Grammar notes

Allocate is a transitive verb, so it needs an object. Common patterns are allocate something to someone/something and allocate something for a purpose. Common objects include money, funds, resources, time, space, staff, budget, responsibility, and land.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The school allocated to improve the library. → ✅ The school allocated money to improve the library.
  • ❌ We allocated for the project more time. → ✅ We allocated more time for the project.

TOEFL tip

In TOEFL Reading and Listening, allocate often appears in passages about government budgets, education, research, city planning, environmental policy, and business management.

Meaning 2 transitive verb /ˈæləˌkeɪt/

To divide something and give each person, group, or activity a particular share.

Natural examples

  1. The teacher allocated one tablet to each group.
  2. The budget was allocated among several departments.
  3. Emergency supplies were allocated according to each region’s needs.
  4. The committee allocated seats to students from different programs.
  5. The available research grants were allocated between the two laboratories.

Nuance & implication

This meaning focuses on fair or planned distribution. The word does not simply mean “give”; it suggests that something is divided according to a rule, plan, need, or decision.

Grammar notes

Use allocate something among three or more people or groups, and allocate something between two people or groups. You can also say be allocated to someone or something in the passive voice.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The food was allocated between five villages. → ✅ The food was allocated among five villages.
  • ❌ Each students was allocated a desk. → ✅ Each student was allocated a desk.

TOEFL tip

When a TOEFL passage discusses how resources are shared, allocate can signal a relationship between limited supply and competing needs.

Word Family

allocation

Part of speech: noun

Definition: the act of assigning something for a purpose; also, the amount that is assigned

  1. The allocation of funds depends on each department’s needs.
  2. The school received a larger allocation for science equipment.

allocated

Part of speech: adjective / past participle

Definition: given, assigned, or set aside for a particular use

  1. The allocated time for the writing task is 20 minutes.
  2. Students must finish the activity within the allocated period.

allocator

Part of speech: noun

Definition: a person or system that assigns resources; common mostly in finance or technology

  1. A budget allocator decides how money should be distributed.
  2. The program uses a memory allocator to manage computer memory.

Common Collocations

allocate money

Meaning: assign money for a specific purpose

Example: The city allocated money to repair old bridges.

allocate funds

Meaning: officially set aside money, especially in a budget

Example: The university allocated funds for new laboratory equipment.

allocate resources

Meaning: assign useful things such as money, time, people, or materials

Example: Governments must allocate resources carefully during a crisis.

allocate time

Meaning: set aside time for an activity

Example: Students should allocate enough time for revision.

allocate space

Meaning: give or reserve physical space for a use

Example: The library allocated more space for group study rooms.

allocate staff

Meaning: assign workers to a task, project, or department

Example: The hospital allocated extra staff to the emergency department.

allocate land

Meaning: assign land for a particular use

Example: The local government allocated land for affordable housing.

allocate a budget

Meaning: decide how much money is available for something

Example: The committee allocated a larger budget to environmental research.

allocate responsibility

Meaning: officially give a duty to a person or group

Example: The manager allocated responsibility for data analysis to two researchers.

allocate seats

Meaning: distribute available seats among people or groups

Example: Seats were allocated according to the number of registered participants.

be allocated to

Meaning: be assigned to a person, group, or purpose

Example: More funding was allocated to rural schools.

be allocated for

Meaning: be reserved for a particular activity or use

Example: Several rooms were allocated for student meetings.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

assign distribute apportion allot set aside

Assign is the closest general synonym. Distribute focuses more on giving things out. Apportion and allot are more formal and often mean dividing something into shares. Set aside means reserve something for a purpose.

Direct antonyms

withhold retain keep misallocate

Withhold means not give something that could be given. Retain and keep mean continue to hold something instead of assigning it elsewhere. Misallocate means allocate something badly or to the wrong place.

Etymology

Allocate comes from Latin roots connected to “placing” or “assigning something to a place.” This history can help you remember the modern meaning: when you allocate time, money, or resources, you “place” them into a specific role, purpose, or share.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. The city’s new budget includes a larger ________ for public transportation.
  2. The exam must be completed within the ________ time.
  3. The committee will ________ more funds to scientific research next year.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The school allocated more computers to the language lab.
  2. The organization allocated to help poor families.
  3. The supplies were allocated among four villages.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. The government decided to allocate ________ to improve rural hospitals.
  2. Students should allocate enough ________ for proofreading their essays.
  3. The university allocated extra ________ to the growing computer science department.
Answer Key
  1. allocation — A noun is needed after “a larger.”
  2. allocated — This adjective means “assigned or given for a purpose.”
  3. allocate — The sentence needs the base verb after “will.”
  4. Correct. The pattern “allocate something to something” is natural.
  5. Incorrect. Say: “The organization allocated money/resources to help poor families.”
  6. Correct. Use “among” for three or more groups.
  7. funds / money / resources — All are natural with “allocate.”
  8. time — “Allocate time” is a common collocation.
  9. staff / space / resources — The best answer depends on context, but all can be natural.
