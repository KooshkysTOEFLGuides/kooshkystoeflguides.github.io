Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 verb / transitive /ɪnˈkɝː/

To experience something unpleasant as a result of an action or situation, especially a cost, loss, debt, penalty, or risk.

Natural examples

  1. The company incurred heavy losses during the recession.
  2. Students who return books late may incur a small fine.
  3. The project incurred additional costs after the design was changed.
  4. Businesses can incur serious penalties if they ignore safety regulations.
  5. The university incurred unexpected expenses when the laboratory equipment broke down.

Nuance & implication

Incur is formal and usually negative. It often appears in academic, legal, business, and financial contexts. You normally incur something unwanted, not something pleasant.

Grammar notes

Incur is a transitive verb, so it needs a direct object: incur costs, incur losses, incur debt, incur a penalty. The past tense and past participle are incurred.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The company incurred because sales fell. → ✅ The company incurred losses because sales fell.
  • ❌ The project incurred many benefits. → ✅ The project brought many benefits.

TOEFL tip

In TOEFL reading, incur often signals a negative result. Look for the noun after it: costs, losses, penalties, or risks.

Meaning 2 verb / transitive /ɪnˈkɝː/

To make yourself subject to someone’s anger, criticism, blame, or another negative reaction.

Natural examples

  1. The mayor incurred public criticism after canceling the project.
  2. The scientist incurred the anger of other researchers by refusing to share the data.
  3. The company incurred blame for failing to warn customers about the danger.
  4. The policy incurred strong opposition from local communities.
  5. The article incurred criticism because it ignored important historical evidence.

Nuance & implication

This meaning is also formal. It suggests that someone’s action caused a negative reaction from other people. It is stronger and more academic than simply saying “made people angry.”

Grammar notes

The object is usually a negative reaction: incur criticism, incur blame, incur anger, incur opposition. It does not usually take an indirect object.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ He incurred to the public criticism. → ✅ He incurred public criticism.
  • ❌ The decision incurred people angry. → ✅ The decision incurred people’s anger.

TOEFL tip

If you see incur criticism or incur opposition, the sentence is probably explaining a consequence of a decision, policy, or action.

Word Family

incurred

Part of speech: past tense / past participle

Definition: experienced or became responsible for something unpleasant

  1. The organization incurred large debts after expanding too quickly.
  2. Extra costs incurred during the trip were not covered by the grant.

incurring

Part of speech: present participle / gerund

Definition: experiencing or becoming responsible for an unpleasant result

  1. The company avoided incurring unnecessary expenses.
  2. Drivers risk incurring fines if they park illegally.

Common Collocations

incur costs

Meaning: to have to pay costs because of an action or situation

Example: Small businesses may incur high costs when they follow new regulations.

incur expenses

Meaning: to have expenses that must be paid

Example: The research team incurred extra expenses during fieldwork.

incur losses

Meaning: to lose money, resources, or value

Example: Farmers incurred serious losses after the drought.

incur debt

Meaning: to become responsible for paying money back

Example: Many students incur debt to pay for college.

incur a penalty

Meaning: to receive a punishment, often financial or official

Example: Companies that break environmental laws may incur a penalty.

incur a fine

Meaning: to be required to pay money as punishment

Example: Passengers without valid tickets may incur a fine.

incur risk

Meaning: to become exposed to possible danger or loss

Example: Investors incur risk when they put money into unstable markets.

incur criticism

Meaning: to receive criticism because of something you did

Example: The policy incurred criticism from both scientists and local residents.

incur blame

Meaning: to be blamed for a problem or mistake

Example: The agency incurred blame for its slow response to the crisis.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

suffer sustain bring upon oneself

Suffer is more general and can be emotional or physical. Sustain is common with losses, injuries, or damage. Bring upon oneself is less formal but clearly shows that someone’s action caused the negative result.

Direct antonyms

avoid escape prevent

Avoid and escape mean not experiencing the negative result. Prevent means to stop the result from happening at all.

Etymology

Incur comes from Latin roots meaning “to run into.” This helps explain the modern meaning: when you incur a cost, loss, or penalty, you “run into” an unpleasant result and become responsible for it.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. The company __________ heavy losses after the product failed.
  2. Many students avoid __________ unnecessary debt.
  3. Extra expenses __________ during the project must be reported.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The company incurred because the market changed.
  2. The new rule may incur criticism from parents.
  3. The project incurred many advantages for the school.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. Factories that violate safety rules may incur a __________.
  2. The organization incurred heavy __________ after donations decreased.
  3. The decision incurred strong __________ from environmental groups.
Answer Key
  1. incurred — Past tense is needed: “The company incurred heavy losses.”
  2. incurring — After “avoid,” use a gerund: “avoid incurring debt.”
  3. incurred — Past participle used after the noun: “expenses incurred during the project.”
  4. Incorrect: The company incurred losses because the market changed. “Incur” needs a direct object.
  5. Correct. “Incur criticism” is a natural collocation.
  6. Incorrect: The project brought many advantages for the school. “Incur” is usually negative.
  7. penalty — “Incur a penalty” is a common collocation.
  8. losses — “Incur losses” means suffer financial or material loss.
  9. criticism — “Incur criticism” means receive negative reactions.
