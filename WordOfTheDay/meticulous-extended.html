Meanings and usage

The core definition and examples are always included. Additional notes appear only when they are accurate and useful.

Meaning 1

Extremely careful about details

adjective /məˈtɪkjələs/ Common in formal, academic, and professional English, but also natural in everyday speech.

extremely careful about small details and making sure that everything is correct

In simpler words: very careful and detailed

Typical pattern: meticulous + noun; meticulous about + noun or -ing form; meticulous in + -ing form

Natural examples

  1. The researchers kept meticulous records of every change in temperature.
  2. Her meticulous preparation helped her answer even the most difficult questions.
  3. Restoring the ancient painting required meticulous attention to detail.
  4. The editor was meticulous in checking every reference and quotation.
  5. Engineers carried out a meticulous inspection of the bridge before reopening it.
  6. The report provides a meticulous account of how the policy developed.
  7. He is meticulous about organizing his notes before each class.
  8. Through meticulous planning, the team completed the project without exceeding its budget.

Nuance and implication

Central nuance: The word suggests more than ordinary care; a meticulous person notices and checks even very small details.

Usual implication: It is usually positive, especially when accuracy and quality matter, although it can sometimes suggest that someone spends more time on details than necessary.

Compared with “careful”: Careful is a broad word for avoiding mistakes or danger. Meticulous suggests a much higher level of attention to small details.

Grammar notes

How to use it
Use it before a noun, as in meticulous research, or after a form of be, as in Her work is meticulous.
What can follow it
When describing a person, it is often followed by about and then a noun or an -ing form, or by in and then an -ing form.
Common preposition
Say meticulous about something or meticulous in doing something. For example: She is meticulous about her records. She is meticulous in checking her records.
Position in a sentence
It commonly appears before nouns such as planning, research, records, preparation, inspection, and attention.

Useful patterns

meticulous + noun

Use this pattern to describe work, planning, research, or another activity completed with exceptional care.

Example: The article is based on meticulous historical research.

be meticulous about + noun or -ing form

Use this pattern to identify the area in which someone is especially careful.

Example: The laboratory is meticulous about recording every test result.

be meticulous in + -ing form

Use this pattern to describe an action that someone performs with great care.

Example: The investigators were meticulous in examining the available evidence.

with meticulous care

Use this pattern to emphasize the very careful way in which an action is performed.

Example: Each object was packed with meticulous care.

Common wrong usage

Wrong: She is meticulous to check every figure.

Correct: She is meticulous about checking every figure.

Use meticulous about and then an -ing form to describe something a person is especially careful about.

Wrong: The scientist conducted the experiment meticulous.

Correct: The scientist conducted the experiment meticulously.

Use meticulously when describing how an action is performed.

Wrong: He gave a meticulous attention to every detail.

Correct: He paid meticulous attention to every detail.

The natural expression is pay meticulous attention to something.

Wrong: The team made a meticulous research into the issue.

Correct: The team conducted meticulous research into the issue.

Research is normally used without a in this meaning, and conduct research is a natural combination.

adverb

meticulously

/məˈtɪkjələsli/

in an extremely careful way that gives close attention to every detail

Relationship: Meticulously is the adverb form of meticulous.

Pattern: verb + meticulously; meticulously + verb

Usage note: Use it to describe how carefully an action is performed.

  1. The data were meticulously checked before publication.
  2. She meticulously documented each stage of the experiment.
  3. The building was meticulously restored using traditional materials.

uncountable noun

meticulousness

/məˈtɪkjələsnəs/

the quality of being extremely careful about small details

Relationship: Meticulousness is the noun form of meticulous.

Pattern: the meticulousness of + noun; someone's meticulousness

Usage note: This noun is correct but less common than expressions such as careful attention to detail.

  1. Her meticulousness made her well suited to laboratory work.
  2. The meticulousness of the investigation increased public confidence in its conclusions.

Meaning relations

Synonyms and antonyms

Only clear and useful relationships are included.

Close synonyms

careful

adjective · Extremely careful about details

Shared meaning: Both words describe someone who tries to avoid errors.

Difference: Careful is more general and may simply mean avoiding mistakes or danger. Meticulous emphasizes close attention to even the smallest details.

Example: The technician was careful when handling the delicate equipment.

thorough

adjective · Extremely careful about details

Shared meaning: Both words can describe work that is complete and carefully performed.

Difference: Thorough emphasizes that nothing important has been omitted. Meticulous emphasizes the great care given to small details.

Example: The committee conducted a thorough review of the available evidence.

painstaking

adjective · Extremely careful about details

Shared meaning: Both words describe work performed with exceptional care.

Difference: Painstaking emphasizes the large amount of time and effort needed to do something carefully. Meticulous focuses more directly on attention to detail.

Example: The fossils were removed through months of painstaking work.

precise

adjective · Extremely careful about details

Shared meaning: Both words are associated with accuracy.

Difference: Precise means exact and accurate. A result or measurement can be precise without involving the sustained attention to many details suggested by meticulous.

Example: The instrument provides precise measurements of air pressure.

methodical

adjective · Extremely careful about details

Shared meaning: Both words often describe organized and reliable work.

Difference: Methodical emphasizes following an organized, step-by-step process. Meticulous emphasizes extreme care with details, whether or not a fixed process is followed.

Example: She used a methodical approach to compare the two sets of data.

Direct or useful antonyms

careless

adjective · Extremely careful about details

Opposition: Careless describes someone who does not give enough attention to avoiding errors or harm.

Usage note: This is the broadest and most direct opposite of meticulous.

Example: A careless calculation caused the final estimate to be inaccurate.

sloppy

adjective · Extremely careful about details

Opposition: Sloppy describes work that is done without enough care and often contains visible mistakes.

Usage note: Sloppy is more informal and more openly critical than careless.

Example: The report contained several errors caused by sloppy editing.
