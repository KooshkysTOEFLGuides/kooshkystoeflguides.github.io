TOEFL Word of the Day

criterion

/kraɪˈtɪriən/

A criterion is a standard that helps you judge, choose, or decide something. In academic English, this word is very useful when talking about evaluation, comparison, admission, research, quality, and decision-making.

June 27th, 2026 @KooshkyToefl Plural: criteria /kraɪˈtɪriə/

Meaning

01 noun · countable /kraɪˈtɪriən/

A standard, rule, or quality that you use to judge, choose, compare, or decide something.

Core idea: a basis for judgment or decision.

Examples

  • The most important criterion for this scholarship is academic achievement.
  • Cost should not be the only criterion when students choose a language course.
  • The committee used clear criteria to decide which project deserved funding.
  • Reliability is a key criterion in evaluating scientific evidence.
  • One criterion by which teachers judge an essay is how clearly the ideas are organized.
  • Environmental impact has become a major criterion for many modern construction projects.

Nuance and implication

Criterion sounds formal and academic. It usually suggests that a decision is not based on personal feeling alone. Instead, there is a standard or set of standards. In TOEFL reading and listening, you may see this word in discussions of research methods, school admission, government policy, business decisions, or scientific classification.

Grammar notes

  • Criterion is singular: one criterion.
  • Criteria is plural: several criteria.
  • Common pattern: criterion for + noun/gerund.
  • Common pattern: criterion by which + clause.
  • Use is with criterion and are with criteria.

Common wrong usages

❌ This criteria is important.

✅ This criterion is important.

Explanation: Criterion is singular. Use it with this and is.

❌ The main criterions are price and quality.

✅ The main criteria are price and quality.

Explanation: The plural form is criteria, not criterions.

❌ We chose the school according to one criteria.

✅ We chose the school according to one criterion.

Explanation: After one, use the singular form criterion.

Synonyms and Antonyms

Meaning: a standard used to judge, choose, or decide

Type Word or phrase Nuance
Synonym standard More general and more common; can be formal or everyday.
Synonym measure Often used when judging amount, quality, or success.
Synonym basis The reason or foundation for a decision or judgment.
Synonym benchmark A standard used for comparison, often in performance or quality.
Synonym requirement Something that must be met; stronger than criterion in many contexts.
Antonym random choice A choice made without clear standards.
Antonym personal preference A choice based mainly on what someone likes, not on objective standards.
Antonym guesswork A decision made without enough information or clear criteria.

Etymology

Criterion comes from Greek kriterion, meaning “a means for judging.” It entered English through Latin. The original idea is still clear today: a criterion is something you use to judge or decide.

6. Short production task

Write one sentence about an important criterion for choosing a university, job, or language course.

Answer Key

1. Fill in the blank

a. criteria — “Three” needs the plural form.

b. criterion — “The most important” refers to one standard.

c. criteria — “These” needs the plural form.

2. Meaning in context

B. a standard for making a decision — The company uses experience as the basis for hiring.

3. Correct the wrong usage

a. This criterion is not fair. — Use singular criterion with this and is.

b. The two main criteria are cost and safety. — The plural is criteria, not criterions.

4. Natural or unnatural?

a. Natural. — “One criterion” is correct.

b. Unnatural. — Correct form: The criterion is simple or The criteria are simple.

c. Natural. — “Several clear criteria” is correct plural use.

5. Best collocation

A. evaluation criteria — This is the natural academic collocation for standards used in grading.

6. Sample answer

One important criterion for choosing a university is whether it offers strong support for international students.
