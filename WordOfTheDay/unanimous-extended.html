Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 adjective /juˈnæn.ə.məs/

If a decision, vote, agreement, or opinion is unanimous, everyone in the group agrees.

Natural examples

  1. The committee reached a unanimous decision to approve the research proposal.
  2. The jury returned a unanimous verdict after only two hours.
  3. The board gave unanimous approval to the new scholarship program.
  4. The judges were unanimous in their decision to give the award to the young scientist.
  5. After a long debate, the students made a unanimous choice to delay the trip.

Nuance & implication

Unanimous is stronger than general or widespread. It means there is no disagreement inside the group. In academic or official contexts, it often suggests strong agreement, formal decision-making, or clear support from all members.

Grammar notes

Unanimous is an adjective. It commonly comes before nouns like decision, vote, agreement, approval, support, and verdict. You can also say be unanimous in + noun/-ing, as in They were unanimous in supporting the plan.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The committee unanimous approved the plan. → ✅ The committee unanimously approved the plan.
  • ❌ They made an unanimous decision. → ✅ They made a unanimous decision.

TOEFL tip

In TOEFL reading or listening, unanimous often signals that all members of a group agree. If the passage says a decision was unanimous, do not choose an answer suggesting disagreement, debate, or only partial support.

Meaning 2 adjective /juˈnæn.ə.məs/

If people are unanimous, they all have the same opinion or feeling about something.

Natural examples

  1. The researchers were unanimous that the experiment needed more data.
  2. Students were unanimous in their praise for the professor’s clear explanations.
  3. The experts were unanimous on one point: the city needed better public transportation.
  4. Local residents were nearly unanimous in opposing the construction project.
  5. The reviewers were unanimous that the book was useful but too technical for beginners.

Nuance & implication

This meaning focuses on people, not the decision itself. It shows that all members of a group share the same view. In real contexts, writers may use nearly unanimous or almost unanimous when one or two people disagree.

Grammar notes

Common patterns include be unanimous that + clause, be unanimous in + noun/-ing, and be unanimous on/about + issue. For formal writing, unanimous that and unanimous in are especially natural.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The experts were unanimous to the solution. → ✅ The experts were unanimous on the solution.
  • ❌ The students were unanimous to support the change. → ✅ The students were unanimous in supporting the change.

TOEFL tip

When unanimous describes people, pay attention to the preposition or clause after it. Unanimous that usually introduces the shared opinion, while unanimous in often introduces the shared action or attitude.

Word Family

unanimously

Part of speech: adverb

Definition: in a way that shows everyone agrees

  1. The proposal was unanimously accepted by the committee.
  2. The judges unanimously selected the documentary as the winner.

unanimity

Part of speech: noun

Definition: complete agreement among all people in a group

  1. The council reached unanimity after several rounds of discussion.
  2. There was rare unanimity among scientists about the importance of the discovery.

non-unanimous

Part of speech: adjective

Definition: not agreed on by everyone in a group

  1. The court’s non-unanimous decision showed that the case was difficult.
  2. A non-unanimous vote may suggest disagreement within the committee.

Common Collocations

unanimous decision

Meaning: a decision that everyone agrees with

Example: The committee reached a unanimous decision to fund the project.

unanimous vote

Meaning: a vote in which everyone chooses the same side

Example: The proposal passed by a unanimous vote.

unanimous approval

Meaning: complete support from everyone

Example: The new policy received unanimous approval from the board.

unanimous support

Meaning: support from every person in a group

Example: The plan won unanimous support from local teachers.

unanimous verdict

Meaning: a legal decision that all jury members agree on

Example: The jury delivered a unanimous verdict after reviewing the evidence.

unanimous agreement

Meaning: complete agreement among all members

Example: There was unanimous agreement that the deadline should be extended.

be unanimous in supporting

Meaning: all people support the same idea or action

Example: The researchers were unanimous in supporting further study.

be unanimous that

Meaning: all people agree that something is true

Example: Experts were unanimous that the evidence was convincing.

almost unanimous

Meaning: supported by nearly everyone, but not absolutely everyone

Example: The decision was almost unanimous, with only one member voting against it.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

agreed united of one mind undivided

Agreed is simple and general. United often suggests people stand together emotionally or politically. Of one mind means people share the same opinion, but it is less common in academic writing. Undivided is often used in phrases like undivided support or undivided attention.

Direct antonyms

divided split non-unanimous disagreeing

Divided and split mean a group has different opinions. Non-unanimous is more formal and means not everyone agreed. Disagreeing describes people who do not share the same opinion.

Etymology

Unanimous comes from Latin roots meaning “one mind” or “one spirit.” The first part is related to uni-, meaning “one,” and the second part is related to animus, meaning “mind” or “spirit.” This history helps you remember the modern meaning: when a group is unanimous, they are like “one mind” on an issue.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. The board voted __________ to approve the new science building.
  2. The final decision was not __________ because two members disagreed.
  3. The committee finally reached __________ after three hours of discussion.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The researchers were unanimous that the method was reliable.
  2. The council unanimous rejected the proposal.
  3. The students were unanimous to support the new schedule.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. After reviewing the evidence, the jury reached a unanimous __________.
  2. The school board gave unanimous __________ to the new reading program.
  3. Experts were unanimous __________ the need for further research.

4. TOEFL-style meaning check

Choose the closest meaning of the word in context.

  1. The committee’s recommendation was unanimous, leaving little doubt about the strength of its support.
    A. delayed because of disagreement
    B. supported by all members
    C. based on weak evidence
    D. accepted only by experts
  2. Although the public response was not unanimous, most residents favored the new transportation plan.
    A. shared by absolutely everyone
    B. strongly emotional
    C. legally required
    D. difficult to understand
Answer Key
  1. 1.1 unanimously — You need the adverb because it describes how the board voted.
  2. 1.2 unanimous — You need the adjective after was not.
  3. 1.3 unanimity — You need the noun meaning complete agreement.
  4. 2.1 Correct. The pattern unanimous that + clause is natural.
  5. 2.2 Incorrect. Say: The council unanimously rejected the proposal.
  6. 2.3 Incorrect. Say: The students were unanimous in supporting the new schedule.
  7. 3.1 verdict — Unanimous verdict is common in legal contexts.
  8. 3.2 approval — Unanimous approval means everyone supported it.
  9. 3.3 on/about — Both are possible, but unanimous on is often natural with an issue or need.
  10. 4.1 B — Unanimous means supported or agreed on by everyone in the group.
  11. 4.2 A — The sentence says the response was not unanimous, so it was not shared by absolutely everyone.
