Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 verb (transitive) /ˈkwɑːn.t̬ə.faɪ/

To measure something and express it as a number, amount, or value.

Natural examples

  1. The researchers quantified the amount of carbon dioxide released into the atmosphere.
  2. Scientists are trying to quantify the effects of climate change on agriculture.
  3. The survey quantified the percentage of students who study abroad.
  4. The laboratory can accurately quantify the level of bacteria in the sample.
  5. Modern software helps businesses quantify energy consumption more efficiently.

Nuance & implication

Quantify emphasizes converting observations into measurable data. It is especially common in science, economics, psychology, medicine, engineering, and research papers. When you quantify something, you make it more objective by using numbers instead of vague descriptions.

Grammar notes

Quantify is almost always a transitive verb, so it normally takes a direct object: quantify costs, quantify risk, quantify pollution. It is commonly followed by phrases such as by using..., in terms of..., or as a percentage.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The scientist quantified about pollution. → ✅ The scientist quantified the amount of pollution.
  • ❌ We quantified that pollution. → ✅ We quantified the level of pollution.

TOEFL tip

TOEFL passages often contrast qualitative observations with quantified evidence. Watch for numbers, measurements, percentages, or statistics—they often signal that information has been quantified.

Meaning 2 verb (transitive) /ˈkwɑːn.t̬ə.faɪ/

To describe or evaluate something more precisely by expressing it with numbers, measurements, or data.

Natural examples

  1. The report quantified the financial benefits of renewable energy.
  2. It is difficult to quantify the value of a good education.
  3. The company attempted to quantify customer satisfaction using surveys.
  4. The economist quantified the impact of inflation on household spending.
  5. Researchers quantified the relationship between exercise and mental health.

Nuance & implication

This meaning often involves ideas that are naturally difficult to measure—such as happiness, quality, value, influence, or risk. Quantify suggests finding a reliable numerical way to describe them, even if the measurement is only an estimate.

Grammar notes

Common objects include impact, benefit, cost, risk, effect, value, and relationship. Very often the verb appears after attempt to, try to, be able to, or be difficult to.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ We quantified how good the movie. → ✅ We quantified how highly people rated the movie.
  • ❌ The report quantified about the costs. → ✅ The report quantified the costs.

TOEFL tip

Academic lectures frequently explain that some concepts are difficult to quantify. Recognizing this phrase helps you understand that researchers are discussing the limits of measurement rather than the concept itself.

Word Family

quantification

Part of speech: noun

Definition: the process of measuring or expressing something as a number.

  1. The quantification of greenhouse gases is essential for climate research.
  2. Modern software improves the quantification of medical data.

quantifiable

Part of speech: adjective

Definition: able to be measured or expressed with numbers.

  1. Productivity is usually quantifiable.
  2. Some social benefits are difficult but not impossible to make quantifiable.

unquantifiable

Part of speech: adjective

Definition: impossible or extremely difficult to measure accurately.

  1. The emotional impact of the event was largely unquantifiable.
  2. Some cultural values remain unquantifiable.

Common Collocations

quantify the impact

Meaning: measure how strong or important an effect is

Example: The study tried to quantify the impact of air pollution on children’s health.

quantify the effect

Meaning: measure what result something has

Example: Researchers quantified the effect of sleep loss on memory.

quantify the amount

Meaning: measure how much of something exists

Example: Scientists quantified the amount of plastic in the water sample.

quantify the risk

Meaning: estimate how likely or serious a danger is

Example: Doctors used the data to quantify the risk of heart disease.

quantify the benefits

Meaning: measure the advantages of something

Example: The report quantified the benefits of investing in public transportation.

difficult to quantify

Meaning: hard to measure with numbers

Example: The cultural value of the building is difficult to quantify.

hard to quantify

Meaning: not easy to express as a number

Example: The long-term effects of early education can be hard to quantify.

attempt to quantify

Meaning: try to measure something that may not be easy to measure

Example: Economists attempted to quantify the cost of traffic congestion.

accurately quantify

Meaning: measure something in a precise and reliable way

Example: New tools allow scientists to accurately quantify changes in soil quality.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

measure calculate estimate assess numerically

Measure is the closest general synonym. Calculate suggests using math to find a result. Estimate means the number may not be exact. Assess numerically is more formal and means evaluate something with numbers.

Direct antonyms

qualify describe qualitatively leave unmeasured

Qualify can contrast with quantify in academic contexts, but it usually means describe the quality or nature of something rather than measure its amount. Describe qualitatively means explain with words, categories, or qualities instead of numbers.

Etymology

Quantify comes from roots related to quantity, meaning “amount.” The ending -ify often means “make” or “turn into.” So, quantify literally suggests “turning something into an amount.” This can help you remember that to quantify something is to express it with numbers or measurable data.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. The emotional value of the painting is almost __________.
  2. The __________ of air pollution helped the city design better environmental policies.
  3. The results are __________ because they can be expressed as percentages.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The researchers quantified the effect of exercise on sleep quality.
  2. The report quantified about the economic costs of flooding.
  3. It is difficult to quantify happiness with a single number.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. The study attempted to quantify the __________ of noise pollution on learning.
  2. It is difficult to __________ the cultural importance of a language.
  3. Scientists used satellite images to accurately __________ changes in forest cover.

4. TOEFL-style meaning check

Choose the closest meaning of the word in context.

  1. The researchers tried to quantify the benefits of early childhood education by comparing graduation rates and income levels.
    A. explain emotionally
    B. measure using data
    C. reject as unreliable
    D. describe without numbers
  2. Although the effect of the policy was difficult to quantify, surveys suggested that it improved public confidence.
    A. difficult to measure exactly
    B. easy to prove legally
    C. impossible to discuss clearly
    D. unrelated to public opinion
Answer Key
  1. 1.1 unquantifiable — The sentence means the emotional value cannot easily be measured.
  2. 1.2 quantification — You need the noun meaning the process of measuring something with numbers.
  3. 1.3 quantifiable — The results can be expressed as numbers, so they are quantifiable.
  4. 2.1 Correct. Quantify the effect is a natural collocation.
  5. 2.2 Incorrect. Say: The report quantified the economic costs of flooding.
  6. 2.3 Correct. Difficult to quantify is a common and natural phrase.
  7. 3.1 impact/effect — Both quantify the impact and quantify the effect are natural.
  8. 3.2 quantify — The sentence means it is hard to measure cultural importance with numbers.
  9. 3.3 quantify — The sentence needs the verb after to accurately.
  10. 4.1 B — Quantify means measure or express something using data or numbers.
  11. 4.2 A — Difficult to quantify means difficult to measure exactly.
