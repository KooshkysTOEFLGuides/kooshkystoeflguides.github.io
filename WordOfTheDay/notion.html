Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 countable noun /ˈnoʊ.ʃən/

An idea, belief, opinion, or concept, especially one that people discuss, accept, question, or use to explain something.

Natural examples

  1. The article challenges the common notion that economic growth always improves quality of life.
  2. The professor introduced the notion of cultural adaptation in the first lecture.
  3. Many ancient societies had different notions of time, nature, and human responsibility.
  4. The study supports the notion that sleep plays an important role in memory.
  5. The museum exhibition explores the notion of identity in modern art.
  6. Some researchers reject the notion that intelligence can be measured by a single test.

Nuance & implication

Notion is more academic and abstract than idea. It often refers to a belief, concept, or way of thinking that may be discussed, challenged, supported, or criticized. In TOEFL Reading, it frequently appears in phrases like the notion that... or the notion of....

Grammar notes

Notion is usually a countable noun in this meaning: a notion, the notion, several notions. Common patterns include the notion that + clause and the notion of + noun/gerund. Do not use it as a verb.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The research notions that stress affects learning. → ✅ The research suggests that stress affects learning.
  • ❌ The notion about evolution changed over time. → ✅ The notion of evolution changed over time.
  • ❌ Many scientists accepted notion that climate patterns were stable. → ✅ Many scientists accepted the notion that climate patterns were stable.

TOEFL tip

When you see the notion that..., look for the full idea after that. In inference and vocabulary questions, the passage may ask whether the author supports, rejects, modifies, or challenges that notion.

Meaning 2 countable noun, often singular /ˈnoʊ.ʃən/

A general, rough, or incomplete understanding of something; not detailed knowledge.

Natural examples

  1. Before taking the course, I had only a vague notion of how ecosystems function.
  2. The documentary gives viewers a basic notion of how glaciers shape landscapes.
  3. Many people have little notion of how much energy large data centers use.
  4. The experiment helped students gain a clearer notion of scientific uncertainty.
  5. Without historical context, readers may have no real notion of why the conflict began.
  6. The diagram provides a general notion of the structure, but it leaves out many details.

Nuance & implication

This meaning suggests that the person understands something only generally, not deeply. Phrases like a vague notion, a basic notion, and little notion are common. It is useful when contrasting basic awareness with expert knowledge.

Grammar notes

Common patterns include have a notion of + noun/gerund, have no notion of..., have little notion of..., and give someone a notion of.... In this meaning, notion usually takes of, not about, in formal academic English.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ I have a notion about how the process works. → ✅ I have a notion of how the process works.
  • ❌ The lecture gave students a notion to the problem. → ✅ The lecture gave students a notion of the problem.
  • ❌ She has notion of biology. → ✅ She has a basic notion of biology.

TOEFL tip

In TOEFL Listening, professors may use notion to introduce a simplified concept before adding details. When you hear basic notion or general notion, expect clarification or examples afterward.

Word Family

notion

Part of speech: noun

Definition: an idea, concept, belief, or general understanding

  1. The theory depends on the notion that behavior is shaped by environment.
  2. The introduction gives readers a basic notion of the problem.

notional

Part of speech: adjective

Definition: related to ideas or concepts rather than concrete physical facts; sometimes approximate rather than exact

  1. The map shows a notional boundary rather than an officially recognized border.
  2. Economists sometimes use notional examples to explain complex models.

Common Collocations

the notion that...

Meaning: the idea or belief that something is true

Example: The results challenge the notion that larger schools always provide better education.

the notion of...

Meaning: the concept or idea connected with something

Example: The lecture focused on the notion of sustainability in urban planning.

a common notion

Meaning: an idea many people believe

Example: It is a common notion that young people adapt to technology more quickly.

a traditional notion

Meaning: an older or long-established idea

Example: The novel questions traditional notions of family and social duty.

a popular notion

Meaning: an idea widely accepted by ordinary people

Example: The article rejects the popular notion that multitasking saves time.

a vague notion

Meaning: an unclear or incomplete idea

Example: Students had only a vague notion of the historical causes of the war.

a basic notion

Meaning: a simple general understanding

Example: The course gives beginners a basic notion of how economic systems work.

a central notion

Meaning: a key idea in an argument, theory, or system

Example: Natural selection is a central notion in evolutionary biology.

challenge the notion

Meaning: to question whether an idea is true

Example: Recent research challenges the notion that aging always reduces creativity.

support the notion

Meaning: to provide evidence that an idea may be true

Example: The findings support the notion that social interaction improves mental health.

reject the notion

Meaning: to say that an idea is wrong or not convincing

Example: The historian rejects the notion that technological change happens in isolation.

have no notion of...

Meaning: to have no real understanding of something

Example: Many consumers have no notion of how complex global supply chains are.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

idea concept belief understanding view

Idea is the most general synonym. Concept is more academic and theoretical. Belief emphasizes that someone thinks something is true. Understanding is closest when notion means a general awareness of something.

Direct antonyms

fact certainty ignorance

There is no perfect opposite for notion. Fact contrasts with an idea or belief that may be debated. Certainty contrasts with a vague or uncertain notion. Ignorance contrasts with having even a basic notion of something.

Etymology

Notion comes through French and Latin from a root connected to “knowing.” This history is useful because the modern word still connects to what a person knows, thinks, understands, or believes. In academic English, it often points to an idea that can be explained, questioned, or developed.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. The study challenges the ________ that older adults cannot learn new skills.
  2. The border on the diagram is only ________; it does not represent an official legal boundary.
  3. The lecture gave students a basic ________ of how coral reefs support marine life.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The article rejects the notion that cities are always harmful to the environment.
  2. The professor notioned that memory is influenced by emotion.
  3. Many students have only a vague notion about how academic research is conducted.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. Recent evidence ________ the notion that bilingual children are slower learners.
  2. The author explores the notion ________ personal identity in digital spaces.
  3. The findings support the notion ________ sleep improves memory formation.
Answer Key
  1. notion — The sentence needs a noun meaning “idea or belief.”
  2. notional — The sentence needs an adjective meaning “conceptual or approximate.”
  3. notion — The phrase a basic notion of means a general understanding.
  4. Correct. Rejects the notion that... is a natural academic pattern.
  5. Incorrect. Say: The professor suggested that memory is influenced by emotion. Notion is not a verb.
  6. Mostly incorrect in formal use. Say: a vague notion of how academic research is conducted.
  7. challenges — Challenge the notion means question the idea.
  8. of — Use the notion of + noun/gerund.
  9. that — Use the notion that + clause.
