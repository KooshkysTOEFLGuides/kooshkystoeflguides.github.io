Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 transitive verb /ˈmɑː.nə.t̬ɚ/

To watch, check, or measure something carefully over a period of time, usually to see whether it changes, improves, or becomes dangerous.

Natural examples

  1. Scientists monitor air quality in large cities to track pollution levels.
  2. The school monitors student progress throughout the semester.
  3. Doctors monitored the patient’s heart rate after the surgery.
  4. Researchers used cameras to monitor animal behavior in the forest.
  5. The company monitors energy use in order to reduce costs.

Nuance & implication

Monitor suggests careful and repeated checking, not just looking once. It is often used in academic, scientific, medical, environmental, and administrative contexts. It does not necessarily mean to control or fix the problem; it mainly means to observe and collect information.

Grammar notes

Monitor is usually a transitive verb: monitor something/someone. Common objects include progress, behavior, performance, conditions, changes, heart rate, air quality, and water levels. It can be used in the passive: The area is monitored regularly.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ Scientists monitor about climate changes. → ✅ Scientists monitor climate changes.
  • ❌ The teacher monitored to the students’ progress. → ✅ The teacher monitored the students’ progress.

TOEFL tip

In TOEFL Reading and Listening, monitor often appears in passages about science, medicine, technology, education, and the environment. Pay attention to what is being monitored and why: danger, improvement, change, or performance.

Meaning 2 countable noun /ˈmɑː.nə.t̬ɚ/

A screen that shows information, pictures, data, or video, especially as part of a computer or electronic system.

Natural examples

  1. The students looked at the monitor while the professor explained the graph.
  2. The lab computer has a large monitor for displaying microscope images.
  3. The technician connected a second monitor to compare the two data sets.
  4. The image on the monitor was too dark, so the researcher adjusted the brightness.
  5. Security guards watched several monitors showing live video from the building.

Nuance & implication

As a noun, monitor usually means a display screen, not the whole computer. In academic or technical contexts, it may show data, graphs, medical information, camera footage, or experimental results.

Grammar notes

This noun is countable: a monitor, two monitors. Common phrases include computer monitor, second monitor, security monitor, and on the monitor. Use screen when the exact device type is not important.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ I bought a new monitor computer. → ✅ I bought a new computer monitor.
  • ❌ The information appeared in the monitor. → ✅ The information appeared on the monitor.

TOEFL tip

When monitor is a noun in a campus or lab conversation, it may refer to a computer screen, a security screen, or a device that displays measurements. Context will tell you whether it is a screen or a checking device.

Meaning 3 countable noun /ˈmɑː.nə.t̬ɚ/

A person or device that watches, checks, or measures something to make sure conditions are safe, normal, or acceptable.

Natural examples

  1. The patient was connected to a heart monitor during the procedure.
  2. The classroom monitor helped the teacher organize the materials.
  3. A radiation monitor detected unsafe levels near the laboratory.
  4. The baby monitor allowed the parents to hear sounds from the next room.
  5. Election monitors observed the voting process to ensure fairness.

Nuance & implication

This meaning focuses on responsibility: a monitor checks a situation and may warn people if something is wrong. It can refer to a machine, such as a heart monitor, or a person, such as an election monitor or classroom monitor.

Grammar notes

This noun is countable: a monitor, several monitors. Common compound nouns include heart monitor, baby monitor, radiation monitor, election monitor, and classroom monitor. The pattern a monitor for something is also possible: a monitor for oxygen levels.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The monitor controlled the patient’s heart rate. → ✅ The monitor measured the patient’s heart rate.
  • ❌ The school chose a monitor to the classroom. → ✅ The school chose a monitor for the classroom.

TOEFL tip

If monitor appears with words like heart, oxygen, radiation, election, or classroom, it probably means a device or person that checks a situation, not a computer screen.

Word Family

monitor

Part of speech: verb

Definition: to watch, check, or measure something carefully over time

  1. Researchers monitor changes in sea temperature every month.
  2. The university monitors attendance in large lecture courses.

monitor

Part of speech: noun

Definition: a screen, device, or person that displays, checks, or observes information

  1. The monitor displayed a clear image of the experiment.
  2. An election monitor watched the voting process carefully.

monitoring

Part of speech: noun / gerund

Definition: the process of regularly checking or measuring something

  1. Long-term monitoring helped scientists detect changes in the ecosystem.
  2. Continuous monitoring is necessary for patients in critical condition.

monitored

Part of speech: adjective / past participle

Definition: checked or watched carefully over time

  1. The monitored area showed lower levels of air pollution.
  2. Participants were monitored during the sleep experiment.

self-monitoring

Part of speech: noun / adjective

Definition: the act of checking one’s own behavior, performance, or condition

  1. Self-monitoring can help students notice their own writing mistakes.
  2. The app supports self-monitoring of exercise and sleep patterns.

Common Collocations

monitor progress

Meaning: check how much someone or something improves over time

Example: Teachers monitor progress through quizzes, essays, and class participation.

monitor changes

Meaning: watch how something becomes different over time

Example: Scientists monitor changes in ocean temperature to study climate patterns.

monitor behavior

Meaning: observe how people or animals act

Example: Researchers monitored animal behavior before and after the habitat changed.

monitor performance

Meaning: check how well a person, system, or machine works

Example: Engineers monitor performance to detect problems before the system fails.

monitor heart rate

Meaning: measure how fast the heart is beating

Example: Doctors monitored the athlete’s heart rate during the stress test.

monitor air quality

Meaning: check how clean or polluted the air is

Example: The city monitors air quality near busy roads and industrial areas.

closely monitor

Meaning: watch very carefully

Example: The researchers closely monitored the plants during the drought.

regularly monitor

Meaning: check repeatedly according to a schedule

Example: Public health officials regularly monitor water quality in the region.

computer monitor

Meaning: a screen connected to a computer

Example: The graph appeared clearly on the computer monitor.

heart monitor

Meaning: a device that measures a person’s heart activity

Example: The patient wore a heart monitor for twenty-four hours.

monitoring system

Meaning: a set of tools or devices used to check conditions

Example: The building uses a monitoring system to detect smoke and gas leaks.

continuous monitoring

Meaning: checking something all the time or without long breaks

Example: Continuous monitoring allowed doctors to respond quickly to any change.

environmental monitoring

Meaning: checking air, water, soil, or wildlife conditions

Example: Environmental monitoring revealed a gradual increase in river pollution.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

track observe check watch supervise

Track is close when you follow changes over time. Observe is more neutral and scientific. Check can be a single action, while monitor usually suggests repeated checking. Supervise is used when a person is responsible for others.

Direct antonyms

ignore neglect overlook

Ignore means not to pay attention. Neglect means failing to give needed care or attention. Overlook means not noticing something, often by accident.

Etymology

Monitor comes from Latin monere, meaning “to warn” or “to remind.” This history helps explain the modern meaning: a monitor watches or checks something so that people can be warned if a problem appears. That is why the word is common in medicine, science, security, education, and technology.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. Long-term ______ helped scientists understand changes in the lake’s ecosystem.
  2. The patient was ______ carefully after the operation.
  3. The laboratory bought a larger ______ to display detailed images.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The researchers monitored air quality near the factory.
  2. The information appeared in the monitor.
  3. The teacher monitored to the students’ progress during the course.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. Doctors used a ______ monitor to measure the patient’s heartbeat.
  2. Environmental scientists ______ changes in sea level over many years.
  3. The school uses regular tests to monitor student ______.
Answer Key
  1. monitoring — The sentence needs a noun meaning “the process of checking over time.”
  2. monitored — This is a passive structure: “was monitored.”
  3. monitor — Here it means a display screen.
  4. Correct — “Monitor air quality” is a natural collocation.
  5. Incorrect — Say “appeared on the monitor,” not “in the monitor.”
  6. Incorrect — Say “monitored the students’ progress,” with a direct object and no “to.”
  7. heart — “Heart monitor” is a device that checks heart activity.
  8. monitor — “Monitor changes” is a common academic collocation.
  9. progress — “Monitor student progress” is common in education contexts.
