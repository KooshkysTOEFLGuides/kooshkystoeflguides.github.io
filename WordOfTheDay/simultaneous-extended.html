Meanings & Usage

Meaning 1 adjective /ˌsaɪ.məlˈteɪ.ni.əs/

Happening or existing at exactly the same time, or almost exactly the same time.

Natural examples

  1. The two earthquakes were not connected, but they were almost simultaneous.
  2. Scientists noticed simultaneous changes in temperature and air pressure.
  3. The company made simultaneous announcements in New York, London, and Tokyo.
  4. The two students gave simultaneous answers, so the teacher could not hear either one clearly.
  5. The study found simultaneous growth in employment and consumer spending.

Nuance & implication

Simultaneous is more exact and academic than “at the same time.” It often suggests that two or more events are closely connected in time, though not always connected in cause.

Grammar notes

Simultaneous is an adjective. It usually comes before a noun: simultaneous changes, simultaneous events. It can also come after linking verbs: The reactions were simultaneous.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The two events happened simultaneous. → ✅ The two events happened simultaneously.
  • ❌ The simultaneous of the events surprised us. → ✅ The simultaneity of the events surprised us.

TOEFL tip

In reading passages, simultaneous often signals a time relationship. Ask: “Did these things happen one after another, or at the same time?”

Meaning 2 adjective /ˌsaɪ.məlˈteɪ.ni.əs/

Done together at the same time, instead of separately or one after another.

Natural examples

  1. The lecture included simultaneous interpretation for international students.
  2. The program allows simultaneous access by many users.
  3. The lab used simultaneous measurements to compare the two samples.
  4. The device supports simultaneous recording from several cameras.
  5. The researchers conducted simultaneous interviews in different cities.

Nuance & implication

This meaning often appears with systems, technology, translation, research, and organized activities. It emphasizes that several actions are happening together as part of one process.

Grammar notes

Common noun patterns include simultaneous interpretation, simultaneous translation, simultaneous access, simultaneous measurements, and simultaneous processing.

Common wrong usage

  • ❌ The app can simultaneous download files. → ✅ The app can download files simultaneously.
  • ❌ We did a simultaneous between two tests. → ✅ We did the two tests simultaneously.

TOEFL tip

In academic listening, simultaneous may describe methods: researchers may collect data, compare samples, or observe reactions at the same time.

Word Family

simultaneously

Part of speech: adverb

Definition: at the same time

  1. The two machines started simultaneously.
  2. The teacher cannot answer five questions simultaneously.

simultaneity

Part of speech: noun

Definition: the state or fact of happening at the same time

  1. The simultaneity of the events made the situation harder to explain.
  2. The study focused on the simultaneity of economic and social change.

simultaneousness

Part of speech: noun

Definition: the quality of being simultaneous; less common than simultaneity

  1. The simultaneousness of the reactions was difficult to measure precisely.
  2. In academic writing, simultaneity is usually more natural than simultaneousness.

Common Collocations

simultaneous changes

Meaning: changes that happen at the same time

Example: The graph shows simultaneous changes in rainfall and plant growth.

simultaneous events

Meaning: events that occur at the same time

Example: The historian tried to explain several simultaneous events across Europe.

simultaneous interpretation

Meaning: spoken translation given while the speaker is still talking

Example: The conference provided simultaneous interpretation for foreign guests.

simultaneous translation

Meaning: translation done at the same time as the original speech

Example: Simultaneous translation requires excellent listening skills.

simultaneous access

Meaning: access by several people or systems at the same time

Example: The database allows simultaneous access by hundreds of users.

simultaneous measurements

Meaning: measurements taken at the same time

Example: The researchers took simultaneous measurements of light and temperature.

simultaneous processing

Meaning: processing several pieces of information or tasks at the same time

Example: Modern computers are designed for simultaneous processing of many tasks.

simultaneous occurrence

Meaning: the fact that two or more things happen together

Example: The simultaneous occurrence of drought and high temperatures damaged crops.

Direct Synonyms & Antonyms

Direct synonyms

concurrent synchronous co-occurring

Concurrent is very close and common in academic or formal contexts. Synchronous is common in technology, communication, and education. Co-occurring means happening together, often in research or analysis.

Direct antonyms

sequential successive separate

Sequential and successive mean happening in order, one after another. Separate can mean not done together or not connected as one process.

Etymology

Simultaneous comes from Latin roots connected to the idea of “together” or “at the same time.” This history can help you remember the modern meaning: when events are simultaneous, they happen together in time.

Practice Exercises

1. Word family choice

Choose the best member of the word family for each blank.

  1. The two alarms rang __________, confusing everyone in the building.
  2. The __________ of the two discoveries led scientists to compare their causes.
  3. The system supports __________ access by multiple users.

2. Correct or incorrect?

Decide whether each sentence uses the word correctly. If it is wrong, correct it.

  1. The two experiments happened simultaneous.
  2. The university offered simultaneous interpretation during the lecture.
  3. The researchers simultaneous measured the samples.

3. Collocation practice

Complete each sentence with a natural collocation.

  1. The conference provided __________ interpretation for international visitors.
  2. The graph shows __________ changes in temperature and rainfall.
  3. The database allows __________ access by many users.
Answer Key
  1. simultaneously — We need an adverb after the verb “rang.”
  2. simultaneity — We need a noun meaning “the fact of happening at the same time.”
  3. simultaneous — We need an adjective before “access.”
  4. Incorrect: The two experiments happened simultaneously. Use the adverb after “happened.”
  5. Correct. “Simultaneous interpretation” is a natural collocation.
  6. Incorrect: The researchers simultaneously measured the samples. Use the adverb before the verb.
  7. simultaneous interpretation — This means live translation while someone is speaking.
  8. simultaneous changes — This is a natural academic collocation.
  9. simultaneous access — This means many users can access something at the same time.
